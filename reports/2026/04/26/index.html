So What

The headline tape looks risk-on: the S&P 500 closed at a record 7,165.08, the Nasdaq Composite at a record 24,836.60, and Japan's Nikkei 225 at 59,716. Under the surface, the global signal is less clean. US and Japanese benchmarks are being pulled higher by concentrated AI/semi leadership, while Europe is already de-risking and macro markets are still pricing geopolitical and policy-premium risk.

The most important correction from the review layer is macro: Brent Jun'26 was verified at $105.33, not the stale lower print rejected in cycle 1, against WTI at $94.40. That creates a $10.93 Brent-WTI spread — a hard signal that seaborne crude still carries a geopolitical premium. That sits awkwardly beside sentiment indicators: CNN Fear & Greed at 70, AAII Bullish at 46%, and QQQ five-day inflows of $6.5B say investors have largely re-embraced risk. The next week therefore starts with a gap between price momentum and macro insurance pricing.

Risk Dashboard

1. Brent-WTI spread vs equity sentiment. If the spread remains near $10–11, the market is still paying for physical/geopolitical risk even as sentiment says risk has normalized. A compression below roughly $7 would validate the equity tape; a hold above $9 argues for caution.

2. BOJ and USD/JPY 160. Asia's key threshold is policy, not index level. A hawkish hold could strengthen the yen and pressure exporters; a dovish hold risks a move through 160 and intervention rhetoric.

3. AI concentration into hyperscaler earnings. US and Japan both depend on AI/semi leadership. That works while earnings guidance confirms capex acceleration; it leaves the tape exposed if mega-cap commentary merely meets expectations.

4. Europe as the early warning. The corrected EU weekly losses are broad: STOXX 600 -2.54%, FTSE 100 -2.70%, CAC -3.17%, Euro STOXX 50 -2.88%. Europe is already trading the downside scenario that US sentiment has not priced.

5. Positioning speed. Fear & Greed rose 55 points in about a month, AAII bullishness jumped 14.3 percentage points in a week, and put protection has been monetized away. The level is not yet extreme, but the velocity increases drawdown asymmetry.

Cross-Segment Synthesis

This is a records-with-premium tape. The records are visible in US and Japanese headline indices. The premium is visible in Brent, gold, European equities, and USD/JPY. The cleaner read is not that markets are broadly calm; it is that investors are willing to chase concentrated growth winners while simultaneously paying up for hedges against energy, currency, and central-bank credibility risks.

The contradiction matters because the next catalysts are tightly clustered. The BOJ meets with the yen close to an intervention threshold. Hyperscaler earnings will test whether AI capex expectations can keep expanding from already elevated levels. Any Iran or energy-market headline can validate or puncture the Brent-WTI premium. In that environment, US risk assets can continue higher, but the margin for disappointment is thinner than the index records imply.

Fed credibility is the second cross-asset thread. Macro analysts verified the Tillis-Warsh-Powell story across multiple outlets: the nomination path may be clearing, but the dollar at 98.51 and gold near $4,741 say investors still see institutional-risk premium. A clean confirmation process could support the dollar; more political noise would keep gold bid.

Conditions

US equities are risk-on with thinning breadth. Europe is risk-off but not disorderly. Asia is bullish but fragile into BOJ week. Macro is risk-off via energy, gold, and dollar-confidence signals. Sentiment is greed, not yet extreme, but fast-moving. The practical takeaway: chase strength only with defined risk; the better cross-market edge is watching whether the Brent-WTI spread and USD/JPY confirm or reject the optimism embedded in equities.

Deep Dive

The single most important data point recovered by the cycle-2 review on Sunday is not a record level — it is a spread. ICE Brent Jun'26 settled at $105.33 against WTI Jun'26 at $94.40, opening a $10.93 differential. The cycle-1 macro analyst had Brent near $97 from a stale Stooq feed; the cycle-2 reviewer caught the discrepancy, re-fetched against CNBC's live ICE quote, and verified the corrected level against five reporting outlets. That correction reframes the entire macro overlay for next week.

A Brent-WTI spread of $10.93 is materially wide. The five-year median sits closer to $4–6, and spreads above $9 historically correspond to one of two regimes: a US shale-supply glut depressing inland WTI, or a seaborne risk premium lifting Brent. The fundamental backdrop rules out the first — US production has been running flat-to-down on capex discipline and the inventory tape does not show a glut. That leaves the seaborne premium read, consistent with everything else on the macro tape: gold near record at $4,741, DXY soft at 98.51, and the EU equity bloc (the most oil-exposed major regional index complex on the screen) printing a -2.54% weekly loss on STOXX 600 with broader losses across CAC -3.17%, Euro STOXX 50 -2.88%, and FTSE 100 -2.70%.

Now run the same scan across sentiment. CNN Fear & Greed printed 70 over the weekend, having drifted from 66 on Friday's close — a +4 point move with no fresh catalyst, attributable to positioning carry rather than news flow. The cycle-1 sentiment analyst's framing of the prior 30 days was explicit: "the Iran ceasefire and Strait of Hormuz reopening collapsed the geopolitical risk premium and pulled the VIX back through 20." Independently, AAII Bullish jumped to 46.0% (+14.3pp w/w) — first time above the 37.5% LT average in ten weeks — with the Bull-Bear spread flipping from net-bearish to +11.6pp. Two independent flow channels (survey + actual ETF subscriptions, with QQQ +$6.5B over five days) corroborate the regime change.

Those two reads — *macro is paying for risk premium* vs *sentiment thinks the risk premium has collapsed* — cannot both be correct. The Brent-WTI spread is a hard physical-flow signal, set by traders who put up margin against it. CNN F&G is a composite of seven weighted technical inputs. Its safe-haven-demand and junk-bond components are closest to macro reality; its momentum and stock-strength components are furthest. With the index in Greed territory mostly via momentum and breadth-extending mega-cap leadership, the composite is overweighting positioning carry and underweighting the macro tape that the futures complex is actually marking.

There is a clean test next week. The BoJ meets April 27–28 with USD/JPY at ~159.5, just below the MoF intervention threshold the Finance Minister publicly named. The Iran-conflict-linked oil-import-bill is part of the yen weakness story; if Brent stays bid above $100 and the spread holds wide, the BoJ has incremental incentive to lean hawkish to defend the currency, which feeds back into Japanese exporter equity pressure (Nikkei's record close at 59,716 sits on a weak-yen export-margin tailwind, with Topix flat and the NT ratio at 15.74 — concentration that breaks fast on a yen rally). Hyperscaler earnings deliver in parallel; clean capex prints can extend US momentum, but soft commentary against $5T Nvidia unwinds quickly.

The asymmetry favors the macro tape. Spreads do not retrace on positioning shifts; they retrace on physical-flow changes — Hormuz reopening, OPEC+ production response, US SPR releases, an Iran framework crystallizing into a verifiable agreement. Sentiment composites do retrace on positioning, and they retrace fastest from elevated levels. F&G at 70 with momentum components dominant has historically gone to 60 on a single 1.5% S&P slip. AAII Bull at 46% with spread +11.6 typically flips back to neutral inside two weeks on a routine drawdown.

The single trade hidden in this contradiction is volatility, not direction. VIX at 18.71 with the term structure firmly in contango means short-vol positions have been cheap to maintain across the rally, and the Equity Put-Call ratio at 0.53 confirms put protection has been monetized away. Buying VIX under 19 with the AAII spread above +11 has been a positive-expected-value trade in most prior analogs flagged by the sentiment analyst — and the $10.93 Brent-WTI spread is the fundamental hedge case for why it works here. If the macro tape is right, vol re-rates regardless of which direction equities move; if sentiment is right, vol decays slowly and the trade pays optionally on residual catalysts. The cycle-2 correction layer did its job: cycle-1's stale Brent print would have framed this week as a clean risk-on continuation; the verified spread reveals that records and the F&G drift are running ahead of the macro mark.
