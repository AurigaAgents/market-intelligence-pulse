# Oil Relief Without a Risk Rally

## The hook

The day's most important signal is that oil fell and equities still could not relax. Brent dropped to $96.58, down 1.26%, as markets priced better odds of a diplomatic off-ramp around Iran and a possible easing of the Hormuz premium. In a cleaner tape, that should have helped imported-energy regions and long-duration growth. Instead, Europe stayed risk-off, Asia sold down after Wall Street's pause, and US futures punished tech before the open. The market got oil relief, but not enough trust to spend it.

## Why it matters

That split tells us the risk regime has changed from simple rally continuation to conditional rotation. On June 3, the US broke an eight-day S&P 500 winning streak after ADP reported 122,000 private-sector jobs for May, well above consensus. The immediate channel was rates: the 10-year Treasury moved back near 4.5%, the level where mega-cap and AI multiples become sensitive. The pre-market spread then confirmed the rotation: Nasdaq futures were weakest, while Dow futures tried to stabilize.

Oil's decline should reduce one inflation tail, but it does not erase the other. Europe shows the problem. DAX, CAC, Euro STOXX 50, and FTSE were all lower in the approved risk-off snapshot. Imported-energy exposure means a day-to-day fall in Brent helps only if investors believe the geopolitical premium is actually leaving. They do not yet. Hormuz headlines can reprice inflation expectations faster than margins can recover.

## The second shock: chips

The other reason oil relief failed to lift risk is technology. Broadcom's weaker revenue print hit the global chip complex, and that pressure travels across regions: US Nasdaq futures, Japan's semiconductor-heavy leadership, and Europe's ASML/Infineon/STMicroelectronics channel. The tape is therefore not just asking whether oil goes to $85 or $100. It is asking whether AI-linked earnings momentum can still carry valuation while rates refuse to fall.

Asia makes that visible. Nikkei fell 1.36%, Hang Seng about 1.4%, and KOSPI roughly 1.8%. The main driver was not local panic. It was profit-taking after the US rally stalled, amplified by USD/JPY hovering just under 160. Japan can benefit from a weak yen until intervention risk turns the same factor into a threat.

## Sentiment confirms the diagnosis

Sentiment is cooling, not cracking. CNN Fear & Greed sits at 54, neutral, down from 60 one week ago and 66 one month ago. VIX rose 3.49% to 16.62, but that is still low-volatility territory. AAII is nearly balanced, with bulls at 36.3% and bears at 37.0%. Those numbers do not justify a crash call. They justify a narrower playbook: less chasing, more respect for levels.

## Our take

The operating range is precise. If Brent stays below $100, the 10-year rejects 4.5%, and Friday payrolls do not validate the hot ADP scare, today's pullback can remain a healthy reset. If those supports fail, the market loses its cushion: tech multiples compress, Europe absorbs the margin shock first, and neutral sentiment can slip below 50 fast.

**Conviction:** Medium. Oil relief is real, but it has not yet become broad risk relief.
