Market Intelligence Pulse - 2026-06-15

Summary

June 15 was a relief-rally session built around geopolitical de-escalation, lower oil, and easier cross-asset conditions into the Federal Reserve meeting window. U.S. equities rallied with the S&P 500 at 7,554.29 (+1.7%), the Nasdaq at 26,683.94 (+3.1%), and the Dow at 51,671.03 (+0.9%). [C-2026-06-15-US-001, C-2026-06-15-US-002, C-2026-06-15-US-003]

The common driver was the reported U.S.-Iran agreement to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. It pulled Brent down 4.8% toward about $83, pushed WTI futures down 4.87%, and helped turn the day into a risk-on tape rather than a defensive flight. [C-2026-06-15-US-009, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-007, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-008]

The macro backdrop did not fight the rally. Treasury par yields edged lower at the 2-year and 10-year points, the 30-year held flat, the 2s10s spread was +40 bp, and FRED showed the federal funds target range at 3.50%-3.75% ahead of the June 16-17 FOMC meeting. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-001, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-002, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-003, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-004, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-005, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-006]

Cross-Segment Read

The market treated the Strait of Hormuz news as a simultaneous inflation relief, earnings relief, and risk-appetite signal. The clearest confirmation was cross-asset agreement: oil fell, U.S. and Asian equities rose, the dollar softened, and Treasury yields did not push higher. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-007, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-008, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-009, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-001, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-002]

Europe showed why the rally was not uniform. The STOXX 600 made an intraday record, but the close was only +0.19%; the day was a rotation into autos, construction, and banks and away from oil and gas rather than a blanket bid. [C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-001, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-008, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-009, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-010]

Asia's corrected ledger matters for the final read. The Nikkei and KOSPI carried the regional upside, while the Hang Seng was up only 0.50% on June 15, not the larger June 12 rebound that blocked the first Asia cycle. [C-2026-06-15-ASIA-NIKKEI, C-2026-06-15-ASIA-KOSPI, C-2026-06-15-ASIA-HANGSENG]
