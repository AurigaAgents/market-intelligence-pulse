# Global Markets - June 15, 2026

## So what

June 15 was an oil-relief rally before the Fed. The market treated the reported U.S.-Iran agreement to reopen the Strait of Hormuz as a simultaneous inflation relief, earnings relief, and risk-appetite signal. Brent fell 4.8% toward about $83, WTI futures fell 4.87%, and the same session saw U.S. equities rally with the S&P 500 at 7,554.29 (+1.7%), the Nasdaq at 26,683.94 (+3.1%), and the Dow at 51,671.03 (+0.9%). The constructive read is real, but conditional. Oil moved lower, the dollar softened, Treasury yields did not push higher, and tech leadership kept the U.S. tape firm. The weak point is that the catalyst depends on the Strait reopening story holding while markets move into the June 16-17 FOMC meeting window with the federal funds target range still at 3.50%-3.75%. ## What drove the day

Oil relief became the transmission channel. The market did not just buy equities in isolation. The oil move, the dollar move, and the Treasury tape all pointed in the same direction: less immediate inflation pressure and less need to hide in defensive assets. That is why the Strait of Hormuz reopening story mattered more than a normal single-market headline. It changed the cross-asset arithmetic at once. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-007, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-008, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-009]

U.S. tech kept leadership intact. The S&P, Nasdaq, and Dow all rose, but the Nasdaq's +3.1% move kept the session from being only an oil-sensitive relief trade. The sentiment segment also described a tech-led rally and a Dow record close, so the equity tone had both macro relief and leadership confirmation. [C-2026-06-15-US-001, C-2026-06-15-US-002, C-2026-06-15-US-003, C-2026-06-15-SENTIMENT-01, C-2026-06-15-SENTIMENT-02]

Europe confirmed the relief, then showed the limit. The STOXX 600 made an intraday record at 641.66 but closed only +0.19% at 634.44. That matters because it keeps the bullish story from becoming a blanket risk-on claim. Lower crude supported autos, construction, and banks, while energy-heavy London lagged. The tape was rotational, not universal. [C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-001, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-004, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-008, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-009, C-2026-06-15-EU-C1-010]

Asia was positive, but the corrected basis matters. Japan and Korea carried the regional upside, with Nikkei and KOSPI leadership. Hong Kong was positive too, but only +0.50% on the corrected June 15 basis; it was not the larger June 12 rebound that blocked the first Asia cycle. The correct read is broad positive Asia with Japan/Korea leadership, not a uniform regional surge. [C-2026-06-15-ASIA-NIKKEI, C-2026-06-15-ASIA-KOSPI, C-2026-06-15-ASIA-HANGSENG]

## Bottom line

The day's message is conditional optimism. Risk assets liked the oil relief, U.S. technology leadership, and a rate tape that did not fight the rally. But the market is not priced for a quiet world; it is priced for one specific risk premium to keep fading. If the Strait reopening story fails or the FOMC reintroduces a hawkish rate shock, the first place to watch is the same place Europe already warned: a strong intraday risk-on move that fades into a narrower close.
