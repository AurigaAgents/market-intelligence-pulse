## Analysis

European equities opened June 15, 2026 with a geopolitical relief gap-up and closed only marginally higher — a record-high session that the indices largely faded into the bell. The catalyst was a preliminary U.S.-Iran peace pact: a memorandum of understanding signed electronically over the weekend (formal signing flagged for June 19 in Switzerland) under which President Trump authorized reopening the Strait of Hormuz. That sent crude sharply lower — CNBC reported U.S. crude last down about 5% near $80, and The Sunday Guardian put Brent down more than 4% to the low $80s, its lowest since March — and drove a rotation out of energy and defensives into cyclicals.

The headline is sharper than the close. The **STOXX Europe 600** printed a fresh intraday all-time high of **641.66**, which Reuters said surpassed the prior record set just before the war, but it surrendered most of that move to finish at **634.44**, up just **0.19%** (CNBC: +0.25%). So the "record high" was real intraday, while the closing tape was barely green at the pan-European level.

Beneath that flat benchmark, dispersion did the talking. Germany's **DAX** led the majors, closing **24,894.01 (+1.05%)** after briefly clearing 25,000 intraday (high 25,085.80). Italy's **FTSE MIB** closed **51,835.95 (+0.66%)** and remains the regional standout on a year-to-date basis at **+15.33%**, carried by autos and banks even as energy major Eni retreated. France's **CAC 40** added **0.40%** to **8,384.01**. The outlier was the UK's **FTSE 100**, which actually closed **down 0.39%** at **10,430.62**: its heavy oil-and-gas weighting turned the very catalyst that lifted the continent — falling crude — into a drag, making London the day's loser while Frankfurt and Milan led.

Sector breadth confirms the peace-dividend rotation rather than a broad melt-up. CNBC reported the **autos sector led at +2.8%** with **construction +1.8%**, while **oil and gas shed about 3%** as the weakest group and **telecommunications fell 1.9%**. Bank strength rode alongside the risk-on tone, with **Deutsche Bank the top DAX gainer at +4.31%**. The composition — cyclicals and financials up, energy and defensives down — is exactly what a credible end to the conflict and a lower oil price imply for European earnings.

The cycle-1 read is therefore a nuanced one: a genuine intraday record on a real de-escalation, but a close that only edged higher at the index level because the move was a rotation, not a uniform rally — and because the energy-heavy FTSE 100 leaned the other way. The risk to the narrative is execution: the deal is preliminary and the Strait's traffic normalization is expected to lag the announcement, so the oil-driven leg of this rotation depends on the framework actually holding.
