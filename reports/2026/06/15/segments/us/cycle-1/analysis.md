## Analysis

US equities opened a holiday-shortened week with a broad, risk-on rally on Monday, June 15, 2026. At the regular close the **Nasdaq Composite** led the major indexes, jumping 795.10 points (+3.1%) to 26,683.94; the **S&P 500** rose 122.83 points (+1.7%) to 7,554.29; the **Dow Jones Industrial Average** added 468.77 points (+0.9%) to 51,671.03; and the small-cap **Russell 2000** gained 21.10 points (+0.7%) to 2,965.09. The day's leadership ranking — tech-heavy Nasdaq out front, blue-chip Dow trailing — was the mirror image of a defensive tape, signaling appetite for risk and duration-sensitive growth names.

The defining catalyst was geopolitical. Stock markets rallied worldwide and oil prices eased after the United States and Iran reached a tentative deal to restore the global flow of crude. **Brent crude fell 4.8%** on the session, and the prospect of a durable de-escalation — a conflict that had worsened inflation globally — lifted hopes for a longer-term fix. TheStreet's live coverage framed the driver as a deal to end U.S.–Iran hostilities and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, with leadership through the session in technology, consumer-discretionary and industrial shares; AI names rallied after their sharp swings over the prior couple of weeks. Cheaper crude also let companies with large fuel bills pace the advance.

Beneath the tape, the move extended an already strong year. Through Monday's close the **S&P 500** is up 10.4% year to date, the **Dow** +7.5%, the **Nasdaq** +14.8% (leading the large-cap indexes), and the **Russell 2000** +19.5% — small caps remaining the YTD standout. The rally arrives in a compressed trading week: U.S. equity markets are closed Friday, June 19, for the Juneteenth holiday, leaving four sessions for positioning around the Iran-deal headlines and the Federal Reserve's June meeting.
