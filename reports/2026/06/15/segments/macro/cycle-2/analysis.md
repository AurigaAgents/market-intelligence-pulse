## Analysis

The June 15 macro signal was a disinflationary relief trade rather than a simple growth-risk chase. Equities rallied hard, with the S&P 500 closing at 7,554.29, up 1.7%, after news of a U.S.-Iran agreement that could reopen the Strait of Hormuz and restore oil flows. That risk-on impulse matters for macro because the strongest price signal came through energy: Brent crude fell 4.8% and was reported around $83 a barrel, while Barchart reported July WTI crude down $4.13, or 4.87%, on the day. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-007, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-008, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-011]

Rates confirmed that the oil shock reversal dominated the day. The official Treasury par-yield curve barely moved but leaned lower: the 2-year par yield fell to 4.07% from 4.09%, the 10-year eased to 4.47% from 4.48%, and the 30-year held at 4.97%. The 2s10s spread therefore widened by 1 basis point to +40 basis points, a mild bull steepening consistent with the front end absorbing a lower near-term inflation and policy-risk premium. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-001, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-002, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-003, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-004]

The policy setup is now sourced to primary official data. FRED's daily lower- and upper-limit series show the federal funds target range at 3.50%-3.75% on 2026-06-15, and the Federal Reserve calendar lists a June 16-17 FOMC meeting associated with a Summary of Economic Projections. That means the June 15 tape was positioned immediately before an SEP meeting, with lower oil relieving one source of hike pressure but the policy decision still pending. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-005, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-006]

Currency and precious-metals quotes completed the same cross-asset pattern. FXStreet reported the U.S. Dollar Index around 99.50 during Asian hours as safe-haven demand faded after the deal news. Fortune reported gold at $4,343 per ounce at 9:05 a.m. ET, up $148 from the same time on June 12. Gold strength alongside a softer dollar and lower Treasury yields points less to classic risk aversion than to a lower real-rate impulse and continued policy uncertainty before the FOMC. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-009, C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-010]

Net read: the macro tape leaned toward easier financial conditions into the Fed meeting. The cleanest evidence is the combination of lower oil, slightly lower front-end and 10-year Treasury par yields, a softer dollar, and firmer gold. The unresolved risk is timing: Guardian's reporting emphasized that the details and timing of the Strait reopening were still unclear, so the market had priced the relief before the physical energy normalization was fully proven. [C-2026-06-15-MACRO-C2-007]
