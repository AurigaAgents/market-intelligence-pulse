## Analysis

Asian equity markets advanced on Monday, June 15, 2026, with the strongest confirmed moves in Japan and South Korea and smaller but still positive closes in Hong Kong and mainland China.

The Nikkei 225 closed at 69,317.50, up 3,297.46 points from its June 12 close of 66,020.04, a 4.99% close-to-close gain on the Tokyo regular-session basis. South Korea's KOSPI also posted a large risk-on move, closing 422.36 points higher, or 5.20%, at 8,545.98 on the Korea Exchange regular-close basis.

The corrected Hong Kong basis is materially smaller than the blocked cycle-1 artifact: MarketScreener's 5-day quote table shows the Hang Seng at 24,842.67 points on June 15, up 0.50%, while its June 12 row shows 24,718.10 points and +1.93%. That makes the June 15 Hang Seng move a modest gain, not a repeat of the June 12 rebound.

Mainland China was positive as well. Xinhua reported the Shanghai Composite Index up 1.61% to 4,096.47 points at the June 15 close. The regional read is therefore broad risk appetite, but with a clear split: Japan and Korea delivered outsized index gains, while Hong Kong and Shanghai confirmed a more measured advance.
