# Memory Boom, Mega-Cap Drag - 2026-06-25

## The hook

The decisive June 25 story was the memory squeeze cutting the equity market in two. Micron's fiscal Q3 revenue reached $41.46 billion versus $35.84 billion expected, adjusted EPS was $25.11 versus $20.78 expected, gross margin was 84.9%, and fiscal Q4 revenue guidance was about $50 billion versus $43.58 billion expected. The stock rose about 15%, reviving the memory and chip rally. [C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MICRON-EARNINGS, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-CHIP-MEMORY-RALLY]

## Why it mattered

The same shock lifted Asia's most direct semiconductor supply chains: SK hynix rose 13.91%, Samsung Electronics 5.29%, Advantest 15.06%, Tokyo Electron 7.78%, and Kioxia Holdings 13.1%. Europe echoed the move through a revived AI trade and chipmaker gains, while healthcare and Bayer's 18.7% Roundup-litigation rally helped carry the STOXX 600 to a record close. [C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-CHIP-RALLY, C-2026-06-25-EU-C1-SESSION-DRIVERS]

But this was not broad technology strength. Apple fell 6.13% after raising Mac and iPad prices, and Microsoft fell 3.23% after announcing Xbox price increases, both tied to memory-cost pressure. That drag left the S&P 500 essentially flat and pushed the Nasdaq down 0.5% for a fourth straight decline, even as the Dow and Russell 2000 rose on broader participation. Hong Kong's Hang Seng fell 1.43% to its lowest close since May 2025 as internet and consumer names dragged. [C-2026-06-25-US-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MEGACAP-TECH-DRAG, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-DOW-LEADERS-BREADTH, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-HONG-KONG-DIVERGENCE]

## The caveat

Macro and positioning did not endorse an all-clear. May PCE prices were up 4.1% year over year headline and 3.4% core while income and spending were firm; Brent bounced on renewed Strait of Hormuz risk; and Cboe data showed index and ETP hedges even though equity put/call was bullish. [C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-PCE, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-BRENT, C-2026-06-25-SENTIMENT-C1-PUT-CALL]

## Our take

The base case is rotational risk-on, not a broad bull-market impulse. The market rewarded direct memory beneficiaries and punished memory-cost consumers; it bought small caps, European healthcare, and Asia chip exporters, but kept hedges around the index complex. If mega-cap hardware pressure or oil-risk pricing intensifies, the Nasdaq and Hang Seng are the weak points to watch. [C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MEGACAP-TECH-DRAG, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-CHIP-RALLY, C-2026-06-25-SENTIMENT-C1-PUT-CALL]

**Conviction:** Medium. The cross-region chip evidence is strong, but the inflation, oil, and hedge signals argue against treating the move as clean risk-on.
