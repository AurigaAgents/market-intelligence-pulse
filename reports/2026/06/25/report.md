# Market Intelligence Pulse - 2026-06-25

## Summary

June 25 was a rotational risk-on session inside an uncomfortable macro envelope. U.S. headline indexes split: the S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,357.49, the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.5% to 25,358.60 for a fourth straight decline, while the Dow rose 0.1% to 51,920.62 and the Russell 2000 gained 0.7% to 3,007.86. The same memory shortage powered both sides of the tape: Micron jumped about 15% after revenue of $41.46 billion, adjusted EPS of $25.11, and gross margin of 84.9%, while Apple fell 6.13% and Microsoft fell 3.23% as memory-cost pressure hit hardware buyers. [C-2026-06-25-US-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MICRON-EARNINGS, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MEGACAP-TECH-DRAG, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-DOW-LEADERS-BREADTH]

Asia showed the cleanest chip-leverage response. The Nikkei 225 surged 4.61% to 72,366.34 and South Korea's KOSPI jumped 5.42% to 8,930.30 as Advantest, Tokyo Electron, SK hynix, and Samsung Electronics rallied on the Micron signal. Hong Kong moved the other way: the Hang Seng fell 1.43% to 23,076.91, its lowest close since May 2025, as Tencent, Meituan, Xiaomi, Sunny Optical, and Trip.com dragged. Australia also lagged, with the ASX 200 down 0.66% to a two-week low. [C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-CHIP-RALLY, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-HONG-KONG-DIVERGENCE, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-AUSTRALIA]

Europe joined the rebound but with a broader driver mix. The STOXX Europe 600 set a Reuters-cash-close record at 640.21, up about 0.8%, while the EURO STOXX 50, DAX, CAC 40, and FTSE 100 all rose. Healthcare was the largest sector boost after Bayer jumped 18.7% on the U.S. Supreme Court Roundup decision, and the AI/chip impulse lifted European technology before that sector faded to a 0.8% close. Defence was the clear laggard. [C-2026-06-25-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE, C-2026-06-25-EU-C1-SESSION-DRIVERS, C-2026-06-25-EU-C1-SECTOR-ROTATION, C-2026-06-25-EU-C1-MOVERS]

The macro tape was not a dovish all-clear. BEA's May release showed personal income up 0.7%, current-dollar PCE up 0.7%, real PCE up 0.3%, headline PCE prices up 0.4% month over month and 4.1% year over year, and core PCE up 0.3% month over month and 3.4% year over year. The official Treasury curve still drifted lower, with the 2-year at 4.09%, the 5-year at 4.15%, the 10-year at 4.40%, and the 30-year at 4.86%. DXY slipped 0.12% to 101.4879; Brent bounced 1.50% to $74.84/bbl on Trading Economics' CFD basis but stayed down 22.58% over one month; gold rose 0.63% to $4,024.91/t oz. [C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-PCE, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-DXY, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-BRENT, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-GOLD]

Sentiment confirmed the split. AAII bullish sentiment jumped to 44.9%, the first above-average reading in six weeks, and the bull-bear spread swung to +8.8 percentage points. CNN's Fear & Greed Index stayed at 26, still in fear. Volatility priced both relief and hedge demand: VIX9D fell 0.83% after the PCE event passed, but VIX rose 1.40% to 18.89 and the VIX9D-to-VIX gap widened to 0.97 points. Cboe options data showed bullish single-stock flow alongside defensive index and ETP hedging. ## Cross-Segment Read

The strongest cross-market signal was not "technology up." It was memory supply-chain winners versus memory cost losers. Micron's quarter and guidance lifted U.S. memory peers, Korea's SK hynix and Samsung Electronics, Japan's Advantest and Tokyo Electron, and European chipmakers. The same cost pressure hit Apple and Microsoft, and Hong Kong's internet-heavy index failed to participate. That is why the KOSPI, Nikkei, STOXX 600, Dow, and Russell 2000 could rise while the Nasdaq fell for a fourth session. [C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MICRON-EARNINGS, C-2026-06-25-US-C1-MEGACAP-TECH-DRAG, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-CHIP-RALLY, C-2026-06-25-AS-C1-HONG-KONG-DIVERGENCE, C-2026-06-25-EU-C1-SESSION-DRIVERS]

The macro read is mixed rather than benign. May PCE inflation stayed high at 4.1% headline and 3.4% core, while income and spending were firm. The official Treasury curve moved only modestly lower, DXY softened slightly, and Brent's daily bounce did not erase its large one-month decline. That combination supports the day's risk rotation, but it does not remove the inflation and policy overhang. [C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-PCE, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-DXY, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-BRENT, C-2026-06-25-MACRO-C2-SESSION-STATUS]

The sentiment layer makes the same point in positioning terms. AAII captured a sharp bullish turn, and equity put/call flow favored single stocks, but Fear & Greed stayed in fear and index hedges were visibly bid. The tape was confident enough to chase specific winners, not confident enough to drop tail protection.
