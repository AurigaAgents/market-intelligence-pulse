## Session Drivers
The day was a decisive rebound powered by two distinct engines. First, a revived AI trade: strong forecasts from U.S. chip giants Micron and Qualcomm after the Wednesday U.S. close - Micron citing 22 billion dollars of committed memory-chip orders and Qualcomm anticipating 15 billion dollars of data-centre sales by 2029 - reignited the global AI rally, sending Asian markets sharply higher (Nikkei +4%, Kospi +5.5%) and lifting European chipmakers; Europe's technology sector, up 32% for the quarter, rose as much as 2.5% intraday before fading to a +0.8% close. Second, an idiosyncratic healthcare shock: Bayer jumped 18.7% to top the STOXX 600 after the U.S. Supreme Court reined in thousands of Roundup cancer lawsuits, making healthcare the single biggest sector boost (+1.5%). The macro backdrop was constructive but two-sided. Oil first extended its decline (Brent about -1.2% to 72.8 dollars) as stranded tankers exited the Strait of Hormuz, then reversed to a gain (about +1.5% to 74.8 dollars) after a report that Tehran planned to charge billions in fees to reopen the strait. U.S. inflation broke above 4% in May for the first time in three years - with PCE in line with expectations - keeping a Federal Reserve hike on the table, and traders continued to price Fed and ECB hikes by year-end per LSEG-compiled data. Domestically, France's consumer confidence rose to 84 in June from a more than three-year low of 82, above the 83 expected.

## Sector Rotation
The tape rotated into healthcare and technology and out of defence. On the leader side, healthcare was among the biggest boosts to the STOXX 600, up 1.5% on Bayer's 18.7% jump, and the technology sector closed up 0.8% after gaining as much as 2.5% intraday (Il Sole 24 Ore put the STOXX 600 tech sector index at +0.9%), led by chipmakers reacting to the Micron and Qualcomm forecasts. On the laggard side, aerospace and defence was the clear weight: Italian defence names fell hardest (Avio -6.62%, Leonardo -3.97%, Fincantieri -2.92%), with Germany's Rheinmetall -1.78% extending the prior session's frigate-programme shock and France's Thales down 2.71% at the Paris open. Banks were roughly flat (the Euro Stoxx Banks index +0.09%, BNP Paribas +0.83%), and automakers were mixed - Stellantis fell 0.95% on weak European vehicle registrations while Volkswagen rose on its Everllence disposal.
