## Overview
US equities drifted to a mixed close on Thursday, June 25, 2026, in a session defined by two scheduled catalysts pulling in opposite directions: a blowout earnings report from Micron Technology and a hot-but-in-line May PCE inflation print on the one side, and a sharp retreat in mega-cap technology on the other. The S&P 500 ended essentially flat at 7,357.49 (-0.73 points, -0.01%), the Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 71.72 points (+0.1%) to 51,920.62, and the Nasdaq Composite fell 118.03 points (-0.5%) to 25,358.60 — its fourth straight decline. The Russell 2000 outperformed, rising 21.23 points (+0.7%) to 3,007.86.

The session's signal is again bifurcation, but with a memory-chip twist. Micron's results — revenue more than quadrupling to $41.46 billion and gross margin near 85% — revived the memory and chip rally that had stalled earlier in the week, lifting Micron about 15%, Sandisk about 22%, and Western Digital about 5%. But the rest of mega-cap tech sank: Apple fell about 6% after raising prices on Macs and iPads, and Microsoft dropped 3.23% after announcing Xbox price increases, both citing the same memory cost pressure that is enriching Micron. That Magnificent 7 retreat outweighed the chip rally and capped the cap-weighted indexes, while broader participation — Caterpillar +5.81%, UnitedHealth +2.65%, and a +0.7% Russell 2000 — held the Dow and small caps green.

On the macro side, the Fed's preferred inflation gauge rose to a fresh three-year high — headline PCE +4.1% year-over-year, core +3.4% — but the readings landed in line with forecasts (the monthly headline even printed 0.1 point cool), so Treasury yields eased, with the 10-year slipping to about 4.40%. Oil reversed several days of declines, with WTI up more than 2% to about $72 on reports of an Iranian strike on a vessel in the Strait of Hormuz.

## Session Status
Thursday, June 25, 2026 was a regular cash-equity and derivatives trading day across all referenced US venues, with no market holidays. The day's path was set by two scheduled events: the 8:30 a.m. EDT release of the May PCE inflation report and the equity reaction to Micron Technology's fiscal third-quarter results, which had been reported after the bell on Wednesday, June 24. Stocks opened higher on the Micron beat and the in-line inflation read, but mega-cap technology faded through the afternoon, leaving the S&P 500 unchanged and sending the Nasdaq Composite to a fourth consecutive decline. The Dow and the Russell 2000 finished higher on broader participation.

## Index Closes
All four major index closes are anchored to the Associated Press close summary, with independent corroboration of the S&P 500, Dow, and Nasdaq levels from TheStreet's 4:01 p.m. EDT close update:

- **S&P 500** — 7,357.49, down 0.73 points (less than 0.1%, TheStreet -0.01%) from the June 24 close of 7,358.22. Week-to-date: -1.9%. Year-to-date: +7.5%.
- **Dow Jones Industrial Average** — 51,920.62, up 71.72 points (+0.1%, TheStreet +0.14%) from 51,848.90. Week-to-date: +0.7%. Year-to-date: +8.0%.
- **Nasdaq Composite** — 25,358.60, down 118.03 points (-0.5%, TheStreet -0.46%) from 25,476.64, a fourth straight decline. Week-to-date: -4.4%. Year-to-date: +9.1%.
- **Russell 2000** — 3,007.86, up 21.23 points (+0.7%) from 2,986.63. Week-to-date: +0.9%. Year-to-date: +21.2%.

## PCE Inflation
The Bureau of Economic Analysis released the May Personal Income and Outlays report (BEA 26-31) at 8:30 a.m. EDT. The PCE price index — the Federal Reserve's preferred inflation gauge — rose 0.4% on the month and 4.1% from a year earlier, the highest annual rate since April 2023 and well above the Fed's 2% target. Core PCE, which excludes food and energy, rose 0.3% on the month and 3.4% year-over-year, the highest since October 2023.

Crucially for markets, the data landed in line with expectations: the annual headline matched the Dow Jones consensus, the monthly headline came in 0.1 percentage point below estimates, and both core readings were in line. AP characterized the report as inflation "behaving pretty much as economists expected." The BEA release also showed personal income up 0.7%, real PCE up 0.3%, and a personal saving rate of 3.0%. The in-line print, combined with an absence of upside surprise, allowed Treasury yields to ease even as the year-over-year rate hit a three-year high.

## Micron Earnings
Micron Technology was the single biggest positive force on the session after fiscal Q3 results reported after the bell on June 24:

- **Revenue** — $41.46 billion versus $35.84 billion LSEG consensus, more than quadrupling from $9.3 billion a year earlier.
- **Adjusted EPS** — $25.11 versus $20.78 estimated.
- **Gross margin** — 84.9%, up from 74.9% the prior quarter and 39% a year earlier (Investopedia: "nearly 85% from under 40%").
- **Q4 guidance** — about $50 billion in revenue, versus $43.58 billion expected.
- **Capital return** — a 15-cent dividend payable in July.

GAAP net income was $28.24 billion ($24.46 per share), versus $1.89 billion ($1.68) a year ago. CEO Sanjay Mehrotra pointed to a severe memory shortage tied to AI data-center demand and to long-term customer agreements locking in volumes. The stock rose about 15% on the session (CNBC headline and Investopedia close section; TheStreet midday +15.78%; the live quote widget's +17.31% is excluded as mutable). Micron's stock is up roughly 700% over the past year, carrying its market cap past $1 trillion.

## Mega-Cap Tech Drag
The reason the S&P 500 and Nasdaq could not hold gains was a broad retreat in mega-cap technology, much of it tied to the very memory-cost dynamic driving Micron's profits:

- **Apple** -6.13% (TheStreet close; Investopedia "fell 6%") — the company hiked prices on Macs and iPads, days after CEO Tim Cook told the Wall Street Journal that "unsustainable" memory costs could make price hikes "unavoidable." Investopedia noted the drop nearly wiped out Apple's year-to-date gain.
- **Microsoft** -3.23% — announced Xbox console price increases (the 512GB variant up $100, effective Aug. 1) amid the memory crunch.
- **Nvidia** about -2% and **Broadcom** about -1% on Investopedia's late-afternoon read (intraday, mutable).
- **SpaceX (SPCX)** fell about 1% to a new closing low (Investopedia).

The pattern is a clean illustration of the memory squeeze cutting both ways: it is a windfall for the memory makers (Micron, Sandisk, Western Digital) and a margin threat for the hardware buyers (Apple, Microsoft), which are now passing higher component costs to consumers.

## Chips and Memory Rally
Micron's blowout "breathed life into a memory and chip stock rally that lost steam earlier this week," per Investopedia. Alongside Micron's ~15%, Sandisk rose about 22% and Western Digital gained about 5% on Investopedia's late-afternoon read. These peer moves are intraday-stamped (3:38 p.m. EDT) and mutable, and are carried at medium confidence to keep them distinct from the AP-anchored index closes. The rally was narrowly sector-specific — memory names rose on the demand signal while the broader large-cap technology complex fell.

## Rates
Treasury yields eased after the in-line PCE report. The 10-year Treasury yield slipped to about 4.40% from 4.42% just before the inflation data (Investopedia), and TheStreet put it at 4.394% by the US market close — its lowest level since mid-May. AP attributed the move to the report showing inflation behaving roughly as economists expected. The easing came despite the year-over-year headline hitting a three-year high, underscoring that markets traded the in-line surprise rather than the level.

## Oil and Energy
Oil reversed several sessions of declines. West Texas Intermediate rose more than 2% to about $72 a barrel and Brent crude gained a similar amount to about $75.50, on reports that Iran's Revolutionary Guard struck a cargo vessel transiting the Strait of Hormuz amid an apparent standstill in US-Iran negotiations — Strait traffic had picked up sharply since the two sides agreed to cease hostilities. These are intraday-stamped levels (Investopedia 3:38 p.m. EDT; TheStreet 2:11 p.m. EDT) with no official settlement figure sourced for this cycle; they are mutable and carried at medium confidence with explicit as_of.

## Dow Leaders and Breadth
Beneath the flat headline indexes, breadth was positive. The price-weighted Dow was lifted by Caterpillar (+5.81%) and UnitedHealth Group (+2.65%) and by strength in health care, financials, and industrials, which offset the mega-cap tech losses. Small caps did best of all: the Russell 2000 rose 0.7% to 3,007.86, closing just above 3,000. The session echoed Wednesday's bifurcation — the cap-weighted, tech-heavy indexes held back by a handful of mega-cap names while the rest of the market advanced.

## Cross-Asset and Macro Context
Across other assets, gold futures rose about 1% to $4,045 an ounce, recovering after dipping below $4,000 on June 24 for the first time since November. Bitcoin traded around $59,500, near its lowest level this year after briefly touching about $58,000 (lows last seen in 2024) and off more than 50% from its October peak of $126,000. The US dollar index eased 0.2% to 101.45, and the Cboe Volatility Index rose about 3% to above 19.

On policy, Chicago Fed President Austan Goolsbee called core inflation "too high" and "trending the wrong way." His comments are consistent with a majority of Fed officials favoring a rate hike later this year, after the Fed signaled that willingness at last week's meeting. The combination — a three-year-high annual PCE, hawkish Fed commentary, and a renewed Middle East oil-supply risk — keeps the inflation-and-rates question firmly at the center of the macro narrative heading into the next session. Gold, Bitcoin, and dollar levels are intraday-stamped (3:38 p.m. EDT) and mutable; this context is carried at medium confidence.
