## Overview

US cash-equity sentiment on Thursday, June 25, 2026 is best described as **rotational risk-on under a tail hedge, with mega-cap tech still bleeding**. The session delivered a fourth straight Nasdaq decline even as the S&P 500 closed essentially flat, the Dow rose for a second day, and the Russell 2000 outperformed. The cross-currents are unusually clean: a Micron-led memory and chip rally and an in-line May PCE release powered the bullish narrative, while Apple's 6.13% drop on Mac and iPad price hikes and Microsoft's 3.23% drop on Xbox price increases - both citing memory cost pressure - capped the cap-weighted indexes and extended the Magnificent 7 unwind.

The sentiment stack tells a two-tier story. The AAII Investor Sentiment Survey, published Thursday morning for the week ending 6/24, leapt to 44.9% bullish from 36.6%, the first above-historical-average reading in six weeks and an 11.6-percentage-point one-week swing in the bull-bear spread. The Fear & Greed Index, by contrast, held at 26 (fear), with CNN and Finhacker now converging on the same value after diverging by one point on June 24. Five of CNN's seven sub-indicators - market momentum, stock price strength, stock price breadth (extreme fear), put/call options, and the qualitative read on the other two - are still in fear territory. The VIX captured the split: the 9-day VIX9D eased 0.83% as the PCE event risk cleared, while the 30-day VIX rose 1.40% to 18.89 on the cash-session tech selloff and the Strait of Hormuz vessel strike, widening the 9D-to-30D gap to 0.97 points. The Cboe put/call pattern was textbook two-tier: equity P/C at 0.61 (bullish single-stock flow, led by chip names), index P/C at 1.16, ETP P/C at 1.38, and S&P 500 SPX put/call at 1.31 (defensive institutional hedging).

The macro backdrop is mixed but not dire. Brent reversed several days of pre-war drift to settle up 2% at $75.26 on the Hormuz strike. The dollar index eased 0.15% to 101.45 from Wednesday's one-year high. Gold recovered from a sub-$4,000 print to around $4,045. The 10-year Treasury yield slipped to 4.394%, its lowest since mid-May, as May PCE printed at a three-year-high 4.1% year-over-year headline but matched forecasts. The latest ICI and ETF.com flow data - both pre-dating the late-week tech selloff - show $83.62 billion into U.S. equity ETFs in the post-SpaceX-IPO week and $55.75 billion into equity funds in the ICI week ended June 17, confirming the positioning context but not the real-time tape.

The regime is therefore not the June 23 unambiguous risk-off and not a clean risk-on. It is a rotational, narrative-driven risk appetite - chips, Iran de-escalation, in-line PCE - layered over a persistent mega-cap tech overhang and an unresolved above-4% inflation backdrop, with institutional tail protection visibly bid in index options.

## Session Status
Thursday, June 25, 2026 was a regular cash-equity and derivatives trading day across all referenced US venues, with no market holidays. The day's path was set by two scheduled events: the 8:30 a.m. EDT release of the May PCE inflation report and the cash-session reaction to Micron Technology's fiscal third-quarter results, which had been reported after the bell on Wednesday, June 24. The PCE release landed in line with forecasts at +4.1% year-over-year headline and +3.4% core - a three-year high but not a surprise - and Treasury yields eased in response. Stocks opened higher on the Micron beat and the in-line inflation read, but mega-cap technology faded through the afternoon, leaving the S&P 500 unchanged and sending the Nasdaq Composite to a fourth consecutive decline. The Dow and the Russell 2000 finished higher on broader participation led by cyclicals and the chip-memory complex.

Finhacker.cz's CNN-tracker also showed 26 (fear) with a data-updated timestamp of "Jun 24, 2026, 8:00 PM ET". Unlike June 24, when CNN and Finhacker disagreed by one point (27 vs 28), both publishers now converge on 26. The composite moved up one point from the June 24 close of 25 but stayed firmly in the fear zone, down 11 points from one month ago. Five of the seven CNN sub-indicators were labeled fear or worse at the time of access: market momentum (fear), stock price strength (fear, NYSE net new 52-week highs/lows 1.40%), stock price breadth (extreme fear, McClellan Volume Summation Index at 1,400.47), put/call options (fear, 5-day average 0.70), and market volatility (neutral, VIX below its 50-day moving average). The CNN "Index Update" banner notes the ongoing McClellan Summation Index methodology change for the Stock Price Breadth indicator.

## Volatility
Yahoo Finance historical data showed VIX closing at 18.89 on June 25, up 0.26 points or 1.40% from 18.63 on June 24, with OHLC of open 18.11, high 19.95, low 17.72, close 18.89. Investing.com corroborates at 18.90 (+1.45%) with a 4:14:46 PM close stamp. The Cboe volatility curve was in contango and upward sloping across all tenors on June 25: VIX9D at 17.92, VIX at 18.89, VIX3M at 20.33, VIX6M at 22.35, and VIX1Y at 23.50. The notable feature is a divergence between the 9-day and the 30-day measures: VIX9D eased 0.83% to 17.92 (the near-term event risk of the PCE morning passing without a tape break was priced out), while VIX rose 1.40% to 18.89 (the cash-session mega-cap tech selloff and the Strait of Hormuz strike repriced the 30-day window). The VIX9D-to-VIX gap widened to 0.97 points from June 24's 0.56-point spread. The longer tenors all eased slightly (VIX3M -0.20%, VIX6M -0.22%, VIX1Y -1.48%), keeping the curve in orderly contango. Front-month July VIX futures settled June 24 at 18.90, a +0.27-point premium to spot that signals mild contango rather than acute stress.

## Options Positioning
Cboe end-of-day options statistics for June 25 showed a two-tier put/call pattern. The total put/call ratio was 1.02, with equity put/call at 0.61, index put/call at 1.16, and exchange-traded products put/call at 1.38. Barchart's S&P 500 index put/call for June 25 showed put volume of 907,215 against call volume of 694,491, a ratio of 1.31. The pattern - bullish single-stock options flow (equity P/C 0.61, well below 1.0) alongside defensive index and ETF hedging (index P/C 1.16, ETP P/C 1.38, SPX P/C 1.31) - is consistent with institutions buying tail protection via index products while retail positioning remains bullish on individual names, particularly the Micron-led memory and chip complex.

## AAII Sentiment Survey
The latest AAII sentiment survey (week ending 6/24/2026, published Thursday June 25) showed a sharp bullish leap: bullish sentiment at 44.9%, neutral at 18.9%, and bearish at 36.1%, leaving a bull-bear spread of +8.8 percentage points. This is the first time bullish sentiment has been above its historical average of 37.5% in six weeks. The swing from the prior week's -2.8 pp spread is an 11.6-percentage-point one-week jump, the largest bullish shift since the run-up to the January 2026 highs. The AAII companion article was titled "Optimism Leaps." The survey period closed before the June 25 cash-session action, so the bullish print does not reflect the fourth-straight Nasdaq decline; it captures the improvement in mood driven by the Iran de-escalation, the Micron-guidance-led chip rally, and the in-line May PCE release. The special question asked about the US-Iran memorandum of understanding's impact on economic expectations.

## Fund Flows

Equity funds took in $55.75 billion (domestic $49.60B, world $6.15B), bond funds took in $16.46 billion, hybrid funds saw $4.68 billion in outflows, and commodity funds saw $1.31 billion in outflows. ETF.com's June 22 weekly article reported $119.83 billion of total ETF inflows led by $83.62 billion into U.S. equity ETFs and $21.66 billion into international equity ETFs, with IVV (+$30.40B), SPYM (+$12.87B), XLK (+$8.33B), and SMH (+$7.44B) the top creators and SPY (-$29.62B) and VOO (-$14.42B) the top redemptions. The flows point to strong risk appetite into the late-week selloff but do not capture the June 23-25 tape action; the SPY/VOO redemptions alongside IVV/SPYM creations suggest structural rotation (share-class creation flow) rather than outright equity abandonment.

## Macro Backdrop
Reuters reported Brent crude settled up 2% at $75.26 a barrel, reversing several days of declines toward pre-US-Iran-war levels on reports of an Iranian strike on a vessel in the Strait of Hormuz. The dollar index eased 0.15% to 101.45 from Wednesday's one-year high. Gold recovered from a sub-$4,000 print to around $4,045. The 10-year Treasury yield slipped 0.59 bp to 4.394%, its lowest since mid-May, as the May PCE report came in at a three-year-high 4.1% year-over-year headline (3.4% core) but matched forecasts. The macro backdrop is contradictory for sentiment: the in-line PCE print and easing yields are risk-positive, the Hormuz strike and the 4%+ inflation are risk-negative, and the cross-asset price action - Brent up, dollar down, gold up, yields down - is a classic geopolitical-risk bid rather than a clean risk-on or risk-off tape.

## Synthesis
The best synthesis for June 25 is "rotational risk-on under tail hedge, mega-cap tech still bleeding." The AAII survey's 11.6-percentage-point one-week swing to a +8.8 pp bull-bear spread is the clearest sentiment signal of the cycle, and it aligns with the cash-session action: money flowed into the Micron-led memory and chip complex (+15% on Micron, +22% on Sandisk), into cyclicals (Caterpillar +5.81%), and into small caps (Russell +0.7%), while the Magnificent 7 continued to bleed (Apple -6.13%, Microsoft -3.23%) and the Nasdaq booked a fourth straight decline. The Fear & Greed Index held at 26 (fear) with CNN and Finhacker now converging, and the CNN sub-indicators showed extreme fear in stock price breadth despite the neutral reading on market volatility. The VIX term structure captured the cross-currents perfectly: VIX9D eased 0.83% as the PCE event cleared, while VIX rose 1.40% as the cash-session tech selloff and the Hormuz strike repriced the 30-day window, widening the 9D-to-30D gap to 0.97 points. The Cboe put/call pattern - equity 0.61, index 1.16, ETP 1.38 - confirms that institutions are paying up for tail protection via index products while staying long the single-stock stories they like. The regime is therefore not the June 23 unambiguous risk-off and not a clean risk-on: it is a rotational, narrative-driven risk appetite (chips, Iran de-escalation, in-line PCE) layered over a persistent mega-cap tech overhang and an unresolved above-4% inflation backdrop, with institutional hedging visibly bid.
