# Global Markets - 2026-06-25

## So what

June 25 was a memory-squeeze rotation, not a clean technology rally. U.S. indexes split: the S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,357.49, the Nasdaq fell 0.5% to 25,358.60 for a fourth straight decline, the Dow rose 0.1% to 51,920.62, and the Russell 2000 gained 0.7% to 3,007.86. Micron rose about 15% after a blowout quarter, while Apple fell 6.13% and Microsoft lost 3.23% as memory costs hit hardware buyers. [1-4]

Asia showed the leverage most clearly: SK hynix, Samsung Electronics, Advantest, Tokyo Electron, and Kioxia rallied on the Micron signal, helping drive the KOSPI and Nikkei to the strongest gains in the cycle. Europe joined through a record STOXX 600 close, supported by chip/AI optimism and Bayer's 18.7% jump, while Hong Kong's internet-heavy tape diverged. [5-13]

The macro backdrop stayed mixed. May PCE prices rose 0.4% month over month and 4.1% year over year, with core up 0.3% month over month and 3.4% year over year; income and spending were firm. The official Treasury curve drifted lower instead of selling off, DXY slipped 0.12% to 101.4879, and Brent bounced 1.50% to $74.84/bbl while remaining sharply lower over one month. [14-18]

Sentiment was bullish in surveys and single stocks, defensive in hedges. The report-date synthesis is rotational risk-on under a tail hedge: AAII bullishness jumped, Fear & Greed stayed in fear, VIX rose while VIX9D fell, and Cboe data showed single-stock call appetite alongside defensive index and ETP hedging. [1, 19-23]

## Cross-Segment Read

The common thread is selectivity. Markets paid for direct AI-memory beneficiaries in the U.S., Japan, Korea, and Europe, but punished businesses exposed to higher memory-input costs and kept index hedges in place. That leaves the report-date signal as rotational risk-on under a tail hedge, with elevated PCE and renewed Strait of Hormuz oil risk still limiting conviction. [1-8, 19-23]
