## Session Drivers
The AP wrap framed the day as a drift rather than a directional move: oil prices eased and falling Big Tech shares weighed on Wall Street, while a rise in Treasury yields added pressure on stock prices. Crude fell roughly 3% in step with negotiations between the United States and Iran on ending their war. The result was a classic rotation-style tape, with the rate-sensitive but tech-heavy Nasdaq the clear laggard (-1.3%) and the Dow and small caps higher. It was also the first session back after a three-day weekend, with markets closed Friday, June 19 for Juneteenth.

## Big Tech and the Nasdaq
The Nasdaq's 1.3% decline was concentrated in mega-cap technology. Per Yahoo Finance's session wrap, Alphabet fell about 5% on concerns over high-profile AI-talent departures to competitors, and Amazon, Microsoft, and Meta Platforms also closed lower. With the largest index constituents down, the cap-weighted S&P 500 slipped 0.4% even though breadth was firmer beneath the surface, as evidenced by the simultaneous gains in the Dow and the Russell 2000. The specific single-stock percentages are carried at medium confidence and attributed to the Yahoo Finance wrap; the index-level closes are sourced to the AP ledger at high confidence.

## Small Caps: Russell 2000 Milestone
The day's standout was the small-cap Russell 2000, which rose 0.8% to 3,004.40 and closed above the 3,000 level for the first time ever. The exact close and daily move are taken from the AP ledger, and TheStreet's session coverage independently framed the day around the same first-ever 3,000 close. The milestone fits the session's rotation: with mega-cap tech under pressure and Treasury yields rising, money favored the broader, more domestically exposed small-cap complex.

## Geopolitics: US-Iran Negotiations
The macro backdrop was dominated by diplomacy. Investors weighed reports of encouraging progress in negotiations aimed at ending the US-Iran conflict; TheStreet reported that a high-level committee had agreed to a roadmap toward a final deal within 60 days following weekend talks in Switzerland. The de-escalation narrative is the proximate reason oil eased, which the AP wrap cited as one of the day's two main equity drivers.
