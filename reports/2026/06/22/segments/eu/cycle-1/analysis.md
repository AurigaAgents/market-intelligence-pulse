## Session Status
Monday, June 22 was a normal European cash-equity trading day, and the segment reports a verified close rather than a holiday read. The session is evidenced by live closing prints with full volume: the Investors Chronicle/LSEG FTSE 100 daily table shows a June 22 row (open 10,363.58, high 10,443.16, low 10,346.63, close 10,437.85) on volume of 1,061,872,669 shares, and the Trading Economics Euro Area and Germany pages carry dated June 22 closes.

A point of asymmetry with the US segment for the same report date: US markets were closed Friday, June 19 for Juneteenth and reopened Monday, June 22 after a three-day weekend, so the US daily move is measured against the June 18 close. European venues do not observe Juneteenth and traded a full session on June 19, so the European June 22 move is measured against the more recent June 19 close.

## Session Drivers
The day's tone was set by geopolitics and oil. Per dpa, European shares rose on positive progress in US-Iran peace negotiations and easing inflation concerns from weak oil prices. Trading Economics added that Iran reported progress in the start of talks with the United States over a permanent peace deal shortly after both sides signed a memorandum of understanding, reinforcing the view that energy prices should stay lower and soften pro-inflationary risks. The de-escalation-and-cheaper-oil mix lifted the broad indices and left France's CAC 40 as the only major decliner, dragged by its outsized luxury weighting.

## Sector Leadership
Financials and miners led the advance. The dpa wrap reported UK lenders NatWest, Barclays and Lloyds each up about 4%, while Trading Economics noted eurozone banks Santander, BBVA and Nordea rising around 2% as eurozone sovereign yields pulled back and improved the credit outlook; the Trading Economics Euro Stoxx Banks gauge closed up 1.00% for June 22. These single-stock percentages are approximate and carried at medium confidence; the index-level closes are high confidence.

## Standout Movers
Infineon was the standout gainer, up roughly 5 to 6%. The rationale differs by source and both are disclosed: dpa tied the move to a humanoid-robot collaboration with VinRobotics, while Trading Economics logged Infineon +4.84% to 85.94 (with a DAX news item at +6.09%) as part of the AI-infrastructure rally. Other German gainers included Zalando (+4.12%), MTU Aero Engines (+1.65%) and Siemens (+1.64%). Luxury was the day's soft spot and the main drag on the CAC 40: Hermes fell about 6%, LVMH 3.59%, and EssilorLuxottica between 2% and 4%, amid muted Moncler guidance and recent broker downgrades. SAP (-1.85%), Deutsche Telekom (-1.80%) and Scout24 (-1.86%) were among the DAX losers. Single-stock percentages are carried at medium confidence.
