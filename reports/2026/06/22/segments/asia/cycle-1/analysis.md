## Overview
Asia-Pacific equities were mixed but had a clear leadership pattern on Monday, June 22, 2026: AI- and semiconductor-linked North Asia led, while Hong Kong and Australia lagged. Japan's Nikkei 225 closed at a fresh record 72,353.96, up 1.55%, and South Korea's KOSPI closed at a record 9,114.55, up 62.13 points or 0.69%. Mainland China's Shanghai Composite also rose sharply, closing at 4,163.10, up 72.62 points or 1.78%. Hong Kong moved the other way: the Hang Seng fell 156.29 points or 0.65% to 23,768.52. AP's broader Asia wrap also put Australia's S&P/ASX 200 down 0.1% to 8,816.10, Taiwan's Taiex up 2.8%, and India's Sensex up 0.5%.

The session should not be read as one uniform regional risk-on move. The core support was lower oil and optimism around progress in US-Iran negotiations, but the winners were concentrated in AI hardware and chip exposure: AP cited technology stocks behind the Nikkei's record close and AI-related shares behind the KOSPI's record close, with SK Hynix up 5.6%. Hong Kong underperformed despite the same geopolitical backdrop, extending weakness in a market that had reopened after the June 19 Tuen Ng holiday.

## Session Status
Monday, June 22 was a regular cash-equity trading day across the referenced Asian markets. The market-basis wrinkle is in China and Hong Kong: HKEX listed Friday, June 19, 2026 as the Tuen Ng Festival market holiday, and the HKEX calendar also marks a Chinese Mainland public holiday for the Dragon Boat Festival on that date. As a result, the June 22 Hong Kong and Shanghai daily moves are measured against Thursday, June 18, while Japan and Korea are measured against their normal Friday, June 19 closes.

## Index Closes
Japan and Korea set the regional tone. MarketWatch/FactSet reported the Nikkei 225 up 1.5% to 72,353.96 while the Hang Seng was down 0.7% to 23,768.52. AP independently framed the Nikkei close as another all-time record and attributed the move to technology stocks tied to the global AI boom. Trading Economics carried a slightly different rounded Nikkei calculation, +1,128 points or +1.58% to 72,379; this appears to be a CFD-tracked basis, so the precise cash close used in the report is the MarketWatch/AP 72,353.96 figure.

South Korea's KOSPI closed at 9,114.55, up 62.13 points or 0.69%, per Yonhap's close item. AP also called the KOSPI a record closing high and attributed the rise to AI-related shares, with SK Hynix up 5.6%.

China and Hong Kong diverged after the holiday reopening. The WSJ historical table shows the Shanghai Composite at 4,163.10 on June 22, up from the June 18 close of 4,090.48, with an intraday range from 4,070.17 to 4,164.42. By contrast, MarketScreener's Hang Seng table shows the index at 23,768.52 on June 22, down 0.65% from 23,924.81 on June 18. AP's wrap puts the same broad picture in simpler terms: Shanghai +1.8%, Hang Seng -0.7%, ASX 200 -0.1%, Taiex +2.8%, and Sensex +0.5%.

## Session Drivers
The macro driver was easing energy stress. AP reported that oil prices fell as talks progressed toward ending the Iran war, with Brent down about 2% to $79.02 and US crude down 74 cents to $75.11 early Monday. LGT similarly wrote that most Asian equity markets rose as easing concern over US-Iran tensions improved risk appetite, while warning that expectations for elevated US rates limited the breadth of the move.

The sector driver was narrower and more important for leadership: AI hardware. AP cited the global AI boom behind the Nikkei record and AI-related shares behind the KOSPI record, with SK Hynix up 5.6%, SoftBank up 1.9%, and Tokyo Electron up 3.2%. This explains why Japan and Korea outperformed while Hong Kong and Australia lagged.
