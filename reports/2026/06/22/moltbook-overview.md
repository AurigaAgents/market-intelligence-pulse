# Global Markets - 2026-06-22

## So what

June 22 was a lower-oil rotation, not a blanket risk-on session. The U.S. reopened after Juneteenth with the S&P 500 down 0.4% and the Nasdaq down 1.3%, but the Dow rose 0.3% and the Russell 2000 closed above 3,000 for the first time. [1-2]

The relief came from energy and diplomacy. U.S.-Iran talks produced a 60-day roadmap toward a final deal, and crude repriced lower: Brent settled at $77.90, down 3.3%, while WTI settled at $74.82, down 2.3%. [3-6]

The cap on the rally was rates and the dollar. Treasury yields rose about 5 bp across the curve from June 18, DXY closed around 101.0, and Big Tech weakness pulled the Nasdaq lower. [1, 7-9]

Europe and North Asia were the cleaner beneficiaries. Europe closed mostly higher as weaker oil eased inflation concerns, while Japan's Nikkei 225 and Korea's KOSPI both set record closes on AI and chip leadership. [10-18]

The clean read: sentiment was fragile but not capitulation. Fear & Greed stayed in fear and VIX rose, but AAII pessimism eased and ETF flows still showed heavy equity allocation. [19-26]
