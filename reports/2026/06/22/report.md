# Market Intelligence Pulse - 2026-06-22

## Summary

June 22 was a live global session after the U.S. Juneteenth break, and the signal was a lower-oil rotation rather than a clean risk-on surge. U.S. cash equities reopened with the S&P 500 down 0.4% to 7,472.79 and the Nasdaq down 1.3%, while the Dow rose 0.3% and the Russell 2000 gained 0.8% to a first-ever close above 3,000. [C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-SESSION-STATUS, C-2026-06-22-US-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-22-US-C1-RUSSELL-MILESTONE]

The common cross-asset driver was diplomacy. The first round of U.S.-Iran talks in Switzerland produced a 60-day roadmap toward a final deal, helping remove Middle East risk premium from crude: Brent settled down 3.3% at $77.90 and WTI settled down 2.3% at $74.82. [C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-IRAN-ROADMAP, C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-OIL-SELLOFF]

That relief did not loosen the whole macro tape. U.S. Treasury par yields rose about 5 bp across the curve from the June 18 pre-holiday close, the 2s10s spread stayed at +27 bp, and the U.S. Dollar Index closed around 101.0 before pushing to roughly 101.17 on June 23. [C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-DOLLAR]

Europe and Asia used the lower-oil backdrop more constructively than the U.S. mega-cap tape. European equities closed mostly higher, with the STOXX Europe 600 near 640 and the FTSE 100 at 10,437.85, while Asia had record closes in Japan's Nikkei 225 and Korea's KOSPI but weakness in Hong Kong and Australia. [C-2026-06-22-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE, C-2026-06-22-EU-C1-SESSION-DRIVERS, C-2026-06-22-ASIA-C1-REGIONAL-CLOSE, C-2026-06-22-ASIA-C1-JAPAN-RECORD, C-2026-06-22-ASIA-C1-KOREA-RECORD]

Sentiment stayed defensive on the surface and constructive underneath. Fear & Greed printed 34.74 in fear, VIX rose to 17.28, and index/ETP put-call ratios showed hedging, but AAII pessimism eased and ETF.com reported $119.83 billion of weekly ETF inflows led by U.S. equity ETFs. [C-2026-06-22-SENTIMENT-C1-FEAR-GREED, C-2026-06-22-SENTIMENT-C1-VOLATILITY, C-2026-06-22-SENTIMENT-C1-PUTCALL, C-2026-06-22-SENTIMENT-C1-AAII, C-2026-06-22-SENTIMENT-C1-FLOWS]

## Cross-Segment Read

The cleanest inference from the accepted evidence is that markets repriced energy tail risk lower without declaring all-clear. Oil fell because the U.S.-Iran process moved from weekend negotiation to a 60-day roadmap, and that helped Europe, small caps, Japan, Korea, and mainland China. [C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-IRAN-ROADMAP, C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-OIL-SELLOFF, C-2026-06-22-US-C1-RUSSELL-MILESTONE, C-2026-06-22-EU-C1-SESSION-DRIVERS, C-2026-06-22-ASIA-C1-DRIVERS]

The limiting force was monetary. A roughly +5 bp Treasury bear shift and a dollar around 101 left the most rate-sensitive mega-cap growth complex vulnerable, which is consistent with the Nasdaq lag and Alphabet-led Big Tech weakness. [C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-22-MACRO-C1-DOLLAR, C-2026-06-22-US-C1-BIG-TECH]

Regional leadership was not uniform. Europe was mostly higher but France lagged on luxury weakness; Asia had record closes in Japan and Korea but Hong Kong fell after its holiday reopening; U.S. breadth was better than the S&P 500 headline because the Dow and Russell 2000 rose.
