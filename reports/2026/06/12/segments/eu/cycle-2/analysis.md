# European Market Analysis - Cycle 2 (2026-06-12)

The European market exhibited strong momentum on June 12, 2026. The STOXX 600 index closed at 633.21, surging by 1.88% (+11.68 points), recovering previous geopolitical-related losses.

In macroeconomic developments, the European Central Bank (ECB) initiated its first tightening move since 2023. The ECB raised its key interest rates by 25 basis points, bringing the deposit facility rate to 2.25%. This decision aims to address inflationary pressures, with the ECBs baseline projections forecasting inflation to reach 3.0% in 2026.

In the currency markets, the Euro strengthened slightly against the US Dollar. The ECBs reference exchange rate for EUR/USD stood at 1.1567, marking a 0.3% increase.
