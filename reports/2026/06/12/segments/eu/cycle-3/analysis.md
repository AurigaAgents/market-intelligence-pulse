## Analysis

European equities finished June 12, 2026 with broad risk-on breadth after oil fell on renewed Middle East diplomacy hopes. The STOXX Europe 600 closed at **633.21**, up **1.88%** on MarketScreener's delayed Switzerland quote table; Reuters rounded the session move to **1.9%** and tied the rally to Brent crude falling **3.7%** after President Trump cancelled planned strikes on Iran and said a deal could be reached as soon as the weekend.

The continental index map was uniformly positive, but not uniformly strong. Germany's **DAX** closed at **24,635.30**, up **1.76%** on the day but still down **0.50%** over five days, which says Friday repaired rather than erased the week's earlier pressure. France's **CAC 40** was stronger on a verified close basis than the prior blocked cycle suggested: MarketScreener shows **8,350.87**, up **1.83%** for the session and **1.61%** over five days. Italy's **FTSE MIB** was the regional standout among the tracked national indices, closing at **51,497.21**, up **1.97%** on the day and **3.22%** over five days. The UK's **FTSE 100** closed at **10,471.72**, up **1.63%** for the session and **1.00%** over five days.

Sector leadership matched the oil-relief story. Reuters reported **STOXX 600 banks** up **4.3%**, led by Deutsche Bank and Societe Generale gains of more than 6%, while **travel and leisure** rose **4.1%** to a five-month high as lower fuel costs supported airlines and tourism names. Energy was the weak side of the trade in the Reuters wrap, consistent with crude prices falling rather than a pure growth-led equity rally.

The cycle-3 read is therefore not just "Europe up." It is a high-beta relief rally after an energy shock: banks, travel, Italy, France, and the broad STOXX 600 bounced hard, while the DAX's still-negative five-day change shows Germany had not fully recovered the week's damage by the close.
