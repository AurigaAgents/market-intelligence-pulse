## Analysis

US equities closed broadly higher on Friday, June 12, 2026, capping a whipsaw week with a risk-on session. At the regular close, the **S&P 500** rose 37.16 points (+0.50%) to 7,431.46, the **Dow Jones Industrial Average** added 353.51 points (+0.70%) to 51,202.26, and the **Nasdaq Composite** gained 79.18 points (+0.31%) to 25,888.84. Breadth was the story beneath the headline: the small-cap **Russell 2000** led with a 0.79% advance to 2,943.99, and only two S&P 500 sectors declined, with materials, financials and utilities pacing the gains. The Nasdaq lagged the tape as pockets of technology weakness capped the composite.

The defining event was the public-market debut of **SpaceX** (ticker SPCX), which priced at a fixed $135 per share and closed its first session at $161.11, up 19.34% — the largest IPO in history, valuing the company near $1.77 trillion. MSCI moved quickly to add the stock to its Standard and Large-Cap indexes, with further index inclusions expected to follow. The debut anchored sentiment into the close and reinforced the session's appetite for risk.

Cross-asset signals matched the equity tone. The **Cboe Volatility Index (VIX)** fell roughly 9% on the day, and the **10-year US Treasury yield** ticked up 1.8 basis points to 4.483% (with 20- and 30-year yields holding just below 5%). The macro backdrop was supportive: oil prices fell again amid optimism over a prospective US–Iran peace agreement, easing a key geopolitical risk premium for US assets heading into the weekend.
