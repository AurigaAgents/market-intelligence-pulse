# Global Markets - 2026-06-17

## So what

The June 17 signal was a Fed hold that still tightened markets. The FOMC kept the target range at 3.50%-3.75% by a 12-0 vote, but the accepted evidence shows a more hawkish path: 9 of 18 June SEP participants were above the current 3.625% midpoint for year-end 2026, the SEP median year-end funds rate rose to 3.8% from 3.4% in March, and futures repriced toward a 2026 hike. [1-5]

The market reaction was concentrated in the front end and in U.S. equity breadth. The official 2-year Treasury par yield rose 15 bp to 4.20%, the 2s10s spread bear-flattened to +29 bp, the dollar index rose to 100.47, and all 11 S&P 500 sectors closed lower. [4-6]

U.S. indexes reflected that pressure: the S&P 500 fell to 7,420.10, the Dow to 51,492.55, the Nasdaq to 26,021.66, and the Russell 2000 to 2,917.98. The VIX rose to 18.44, but the accepted sentiment segment treats the move as controlled de-risking rather than panic. [4, 7-10]

Outside the U.S., the tape was more split than the headline Fed story suggests. Europe closed before the Fed with the STOXX Europe 600 at a fresh 639.31 record, supported by banks and technology but checked by autos after BMW's warning; Asia had three positive tracked closes, led by KOSPI's record, while the Hang Seng fell. [11-18]
