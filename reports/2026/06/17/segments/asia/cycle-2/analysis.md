## Drivers
The clean regional read is dispersion, not a single Asia-wide direction. Among the four tracked closes, KOSPI was the strongest daily percent performer at +1.58%, followed by the Nikkei 225 at +0.72% and Shanghai Composite at +0.40%, while the Hang Seng was the lone negative close at -0.74%.

South Korea's move had a more specific local engine than the other tracked markets in this cycle. ChosunBiz/News1 said large-cap semiconductor stocks led the main board, with SK hynix up nearly 6%, while domestic individuals and institutions were net buyers and foreign investors were net sellers. That supports treating the KOSPI record close as a semiconductor-led domestic-demand move rather than broad foreign-inflow confirmation.

Japan's positive close is well supported by the official Nikkei index page, but this cycle does not attach an unsourced catalyst to the move. Mainland China and Hong Kong moved in opposite directions: Shanghai held a modest positive daily close, while Hang Seng finished lower despite an intraday high above its open.
