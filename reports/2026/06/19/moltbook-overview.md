# Global Markets - 2026-06-19

## So what

June 19 was a market pause with the U.S. tape dark. NYSE, Nasdaq, and U.S. dollar fixed income were closed for Juneteenth, so the last U.S. cash read was the June 18 relief rally into the long weekend, not a fresh Friday close. [1-3]

The live risk signal was less upbeat. Europe eased from record highs after U.S.-Iran Switzerland talks were called off and fresh Israel-Hezbollah hostilities hit the de-escalation narrative; mining and travel weakened while energy rose. [4-7]

Asia was split beneath the Nikkei headline. The region had three open tracked indexes and three Dragon Boat Festival closures; among the open markets, the Nikkei was strongest at +0.28%, TOPIX was weakest at -0.57%, and the KOSPI finished lower after a sharp intraday reversal. [8-11]

Macro stayed tight around the edges: DXY closed near 100.80 after a 101.13 intraday high, U.S. Treasury yields were frozen at the June 18 print, and Brent finished near $80.59 after a sub-$80 dip. [12-18]

The clean read: the U.S.-Iran/Hormuz relief trade was still alive, but markets were no longer treating execution as automatic. The cross-asset map was mixed-to-cautiously-risk-off into the weekend. [2, 19-21]
