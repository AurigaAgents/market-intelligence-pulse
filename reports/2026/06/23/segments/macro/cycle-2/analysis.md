## Analysis

June 23's macro tape was defensive, but the stress did not come through one clean channel. Treasury yields fell, led by the front end: the official par-yield curve put 2Y at 4.16%, down 8 bp from June 22, while 10Y slipped 1 bp to 4.50% and 30Y slipped 1 bp to 4.94%. That widened 2s10s to +34 bp from +27 bp. CNBC's intraday rates report pointed in the same direction, saying yields fell despite rate-hike worries hitting technology stocks and with PCE inflation due later in the week. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C2-TREASURY-CURVE]

The dollar did not behave like a market dismissing the Fed. Reuters reported that DXY rose 0.38% to 101.39 after touching 101.42, the highest since May 2025. The same report put CME FedWatch odds of at least a 25 bp July hike at 36.3%, up from 8.5% a week earlier, and September hike odds at 69.1%, up from 29.1%. The dollar therefore carried both policy repricing and safety demand from the megacap selloff. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C2-DOLLAR-HIKE-BETS]

Oil supplied the disinflationary offset, and this cycle uses dated settlement reporting instead of mutable live quote pages. Reuters reported Brent futures down $0.82, or 1.1%, at $77.08/bbl and WTI down $0.65, or 0.9%, at $73.21/bbl, with both benchmarks hitting near-four-month lows during the session. The driver was not weak demand alone: investors were watching crude flows through the Strait of Hormuz after U.S.-Iran peace-talk progress, a 60-day sanctions waiver, and Oman-Iran talks on navigation administration. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C2-OIL-SETTLEMENT]

Equity concentration was the risk impulse. Reuters said the Nasdaq Composite was down 1.7%, wiping out around $776 billion in market value, while the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell 7.5%, Micron lost 10.8%, and Nvidia fell 3.4%. The article tied the move to unwinding technology inflows, rate concerns, and doubts over AI spending returns. That explains why front-end Treasuries could rally while the dollar rose: the market bought some duration for safety, but did not abandon the higher-for-longer policy risk. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C2-TECH-RISK]

Net read: June 23 was a defensive cross-asset rotation. Lower oil reduced the immediate inflation impulse, but it did not erase hawkish Fed repricing. The rates curve bull-steepened from the front end, the dollar strengthened on policy and safety demand, and technology concentration stress carried the risk-off move. For the macro segment, the important distinction is that cheaper crude was an offset, not the dominant signal. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C2-SYNTHESIS]
