## Analysis

June 23's macro tape was defensive, and unlike cycle 2 the equity leg is now anchored to final closing values rather than a mid-session snapshot. Treasury yields fell, led by the front end: the official par-yield curve put 2Y at 4.16%, down 8 bp from June 22, while 10Y and 30Y each slipped 1 bp to 4.50% and 4.94%. That widened 2s10s to +34 bp from +27 bp - a front-end-led bull steepening, not a long-end duration grab. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-TREASURY-CURVE]

The dollar did not behave like a market dismissing the Fed. Reuters reported DXY rose 0.38% to 101.39 after touching 101.42, its highest since May 2025, with the euro down 0.41% at $1.138. CME FedWatch odds of at least a 25 bp July hike were 36.3%, up from 8.5% a week earlier, and September odds 69.1%, up from 29.1%. The greenback carried both policy repricing under new Chair Kevin Warsh and safety demand from the megacap selloff. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-DOLLAR]

Oil supplied the disinflationary offset through dated settlement reporting. Reuters reported Brent settled down $0.82, or 1.1%, at $77.08/bbl and WTI down $0.65, or 0.9%, at $73.21/bbl, with both benchmarks at near-four-month lows. The driver was supply-route normalization: investors watched crude flows resume through the Strait of Hormuz after U.S.-Iran peace-talk progress, a 60-day sanctions waiver, and Oman-Iran talks on navigation administration. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-OIL-SETTLEMENT]

Equity concentration was the risk impulse, and it deepened into the bell. At the close the Nasdaq Composite was down 579.56 points, or 2.21%, to 25,587.04; the S&P 500 lost 1.44% to 7,365.47; the Dow was nearly flat, down 47.22 points, or 0.09%, to 51,665.49. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index tumbled 7.9% and the S&P 500 IT sector slipped 3.7%. Nvidia fell 4.1%, Micron and SanDisk each fell around 13%, and Intel, Marvell and AMD lost between 5.8% and 9.4%. The VIX rose 2.23 points to 19.52, and six of eleven S&P 500 sectors still rose, led by consumer staples at +1.8% - a classic defensive rotation. NBC News and Investopedia corroborate the same closing moves. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-EQUITY-CLOSE]

Net read: June 23 was a defensive cross-asset rotation. Lower oil reduced the immediate inflation impulse but did not erase hawkish Fed repricing. The rates curve bull-steepened from the front end, the dollar strengthened on policy and safety demand, and technology and semiconductor stress carried the risk-off move while defensives outperformed. For the macro segment, cheaper crude was an offset, not the dominant signal. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-SYNTHESIS]
