## Overview
US equities sold off on Tuesday, June 23, 2026, in a sharp, technology-led decline that began overseas. The Associated Press closing-index ledger reported the S&P 500 down 107.33 points (1.4%) to 7,365.46, the Nasdaq Composite down 579.56 points (2.2%) to 25,587.04, the Dow Jones Industrial Average down 45.87 points (0.1%) to 51,666.84, and the Russell 2000 down 28.92 points (about 1%) to 2,975.48. Daily moves are measured against the prior regular session of Monday, June 22. The split between the tech-heavy Nasdaq (-2.2%) and the tech-light Dow (-0.1%) defines the day: a rotation out of semiconductors and AI-linked names rather than a broad capitulation, with the S&P 500 giving back its early-June record run. This is a normal trading session, so the segment carries verified closes plus the day's drivers, not a no-session holiday read.

## Session Drivers
The selloff originated overseas and rolled west. South Korea's Kospi plunged 9.99% — its steepest one-day drop in more than three months — after regulators cautioned that the semiconductor rally had become overheated, and memory-chip giants Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix each fell more than 12%, triggering an automatic 20-minute trading halt. That rout fed straight into US semiconductors and AI-linked names. Yahoo Finance framed the US session as the AI trade cooling off with chips retreating; TheStreet cited Hargreaves Lansdown strategist Anna Macdonald attributing the trigger to Broadcom's results failing to deliver the guidance boost markets had expected. The AP noted that the selling was concentrated in the AI-related companies that had powered the market to record after record into early June.

## Semiconductors and the Nasdaq
The Nasdaq's 2.2% decline was a chip story. The AP ledger reported Micron Technology — which is scheduled to report earnings the next day — down 13.2% on the session. Intraday readings from TheStreet's live blog showed broad chip weakness: Qualcomm down roughly 8.6%, Arm Holdings about 8.4%, Taiwan Semiconductor about 5.2%, and SanDisk about 11.7%, with Nvidia off about 2.7%. Those single-stock percentages other than the Micron close are intraday snapshots and are carried at medium confidence because they may differ from final closes. With the largest growth constituents under pressure, the cap-weighted S&P 500 fell 1.4% and the S&P 500 technology sector dropped roughly 4.1% intraday.

## Small Caps: Russell 2000 Gives Back 3,000
The prior session's headline reversed. The Russell 2000, which had closed above 3,000 for the first time ever on June 22 (3,004.40), fell 28.92 points to 2,975.48 and slipped back below the milestone in a single day. The exact close and daily move are taken from the AP ledger; the giveback fits the day's risk-off character, as the broad tech-and-AI selloff dragged even the more domestically exposed small-cap complex lower.

## Macro: Rates, Inflation, and Yields
A macro overhang amplified the move. TheStreet highlighted a Bank of America research note portending no rate cuts until 2028 and as many as three rate hikes this year, and the AP framed the session around investor worry that a growing likelihood of rate hikes to fight inflation will weigh on economic growth. The 10-year Treasury yield sat around 4.495% and the 2-year reached its highest level since February 2025, even as oil prices fell. The yield levels and the BofA note are intraday reporting and are carried at medium confidence.

## Breadth and Rotation
Beneath the headline, the decline was narrow rather than universal. TheStreet reported that roughly 299 of the S&P 500's 500 holdings traded higher even as the index fell, because the technology sector's ~4.1% drop outweighed gains nearly everywhere else; the Dow's lighter technology weighting limited it to a 0.1% loss. Defensive and software names, plus some mega-cap technology, bucked the slide: Microsoft was up about 2.5% and Amazon about 1.7% intraday, while IBM (+4.2%), Accenture (+3.3%), and Public Storage (+4.4%) were higher premarket. These single-stock figures are intraday or premarket snapshots and are carried at medium confidence.
