## Overview
Asian equities reversed from the prior record-setting tone on Tuesday, June 23, 2026, as the AI and semiconductor trade became the pressure point. The cleanest closing data show the stress concentrated in South Korea and Japan: South Korea's KOSPI fell 910.71 points, or 9.99%, to 8,203.84 at the regular-session close, while Japan's Nikkei Average fell 2,565.58 points to 69,788.38 and TOPIX fell 104.67 points to 3,990.38 at the Tokyo close. Regional reporting from The Business Times said MSCI's Asia equity gauge declined more than 2% after closing at a record high the prior day, while Reuters later confirmed the global tech selloff, the 10% Korea drop, and subdued oil below $80 per barrel.

The cycle-2 source trail deliberately avoids the unsupported cycle-1 BofA and Broadcom claims. The segment reads the Asia move as a regional AI/semiconductor profit-taking shock, amplified by Korea's heavy chip weighting and by yen-sensitive Japan, with lower oil providing macro relief but not enough to offset equity de-risking.

## Regional Read
The Asian session was not a generic macro selloff; it was a concentrated unwind of the year's most crowded technology leadership. The Business Times reported that MSCI's regional equity gauge declined more than 2% after closing at a record high, while the regional Asian technology sub-gauge ended an eight-day winning run. That basis matters: the drop followed an extended rally, not a weak starting point, so the risk signal is positioning and valuation sensitivity rather than broad economic deterioration.

South Korea was the center of gravity. UPI's Yonhap-syndicated report put the KOSPI close at 8,203.84, down 910.71 points or 9.99%, with the Korea Exchange triggering a market-wide 20-minute circuit breaker at about 2:33 p.m. after a drop of more than 8%. Anadolu independently reported the same KOSPI close, point move, percentage move, heavy turnover, and sharp large-cap losses. The halt confirms that this was a disorderly local session, not just a normal retracement from highs.

## Korea: Chips and Flows
The Korea move was led by the same semiconductor complex that had carried the region higher. Samsung Electronics fell 12.31% and SK Hynix fell 12.47% at the Seoul close. UPI/Yonhap reported foreign and institutional net selling of 4.13 trillion won and 4.55 trillion won, respectively, against 8.58 trillion won of retail net buying. That flow split supports a forced-risk-reduction reading: overseas and institutional investors de-risked chip exposure while domestic retail absorbed part of the supply.

The broader market damage was also visible outside chips. Anadolu reported decliners outnumbering gainers by 856 to 46, Samsung Electro-Mechanics down 9.6%, Hyundai Motor down 12.05%, and LG Energy Solution down 6.1%. The Korean won weakened to 1,539.1 per dollar as of 3:30 p.m. local time. These details keep the Korea claim high confidence because the close, halt, large-cap moves, and currency basis are mutually corroborated.

## Japan: Record Rally Gives Back Ground
Japan also gave back part of the prior record run. Jiji Press reported the Nikkei Average at 69,788.38, down 2,565.58 points, and TOPIX at 3,990.38, down 104.67 points, as of the 15:30 JST close. Implied from Jiji's point changes and closes, the Nikkei move was about -3.55% and the TOPIX move about -2.56% versus the prior close. The point losses align with the regional AI/tech unwind, while the yen near long-term lows in Reuters' global-market wrap adds macro sensitivity around Japan but is not used here as a stand-alone trading driver.

## Oil and Macro Cross-Current
Oil moved in the opposite direction from equity risk. Trading Economics put Brent at 76.92 USD/Bbl on June 23, down 1.26% from the previous day, and described the price near $77 as the lowest in nearly three months. Reuters similarly reported Brent below $80 as tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz increased and physical prices neared pre-conflict levels. Lower oil normally helps Asia's import-heavy economies, but this session's equity tape shows the AI and semiconductor unwind dominating the oil relief.
