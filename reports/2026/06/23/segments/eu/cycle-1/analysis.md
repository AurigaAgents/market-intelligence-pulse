## Overview
European equities traded a normal regular session on Tuesday, June 23, 2026, and closed broadly lower in a tech-led risk-off move. The pan-European STOXX Europe 600 fell 0.72% to 634.64, the EURO STOXX 50 dropped 1.25% to 6,232.46, and France's CAC 40 lost 0.71% to 8,340.71 - a third straight down session. The UK's FTSE 100 was the clear relative outperformer, down just 9.00 points (-0.09%) to a precise 10,428.85. Germany's DAX carries a disclosed vendor discrepancy: the Trading Economics CFD-tracked DE40 closed 24,893.58, off 0.98% from 25,139.69, while the Reuters European cash-close wrap put the DAX at only -0.3%. The session was driven by a global technology selloff on AI-spending and valuation fears, amplified by rising bets on imminent US Federal Reserve rate hikes; chipmakers were hammered (STMicroelectronics about -8%, Infineon about -6%), while defensives - healthcare and food and beverage - rose. Unlike the prior report date, there is no US-holiday asymmetry: both European and US venues traded a normal session, and Europe followed weakness on Wall Street.

## Session Status
Tuesday, June 23 was a normal European cash-equity trading day, and the segment reports a verified close rather than a holiday read. The session is evidenced by live closing prints with full volume: the Investors Chronicle/LSEG FTSE 100 daily table shows a June 23 row (open 10,438.24, high 10,462.19, low 10,332.40, close 10,428.85) on volume of 886,362,424 shares, and the Trading Economics Euro Area, Germany and France pages carry dated June 23 closes.

There is no cross-market calendar asymmetry on this report date. Both European and US venues traded a normal regular session, so the European daily moves are measured against the immediately prior Monday, June 22 close. The drivers were global rather than regional: European equities tracked weakness on Wall Street and a build in expectations for imminent US Federal Reserve rate hikes.

## Index Closes
European equities closed broadly lower, led by the rate-sensitive and tech-heavy continental benchmarks, while London held up far better:

- **STOXX Europe 600** - 634.64, down 4.63 points (-0.72%) on the dated Trading Economics EU600 table. The figure is corroborated across vendors: the Reuters close wrap reported the STOXX 600 down 0.7%, and the MarketScreener index widget showed 634.63, -0.73% (from a prior 639.27). An early Trading Economics news line characterised the move as a softer "-0.5% to 636"; that intraday figure is superseded by the dated -0.72% close and is noted only for transparency.
- **EURO STOXX 50** - 6,232.46, down 78.86 points (-1.25%) from 6,311.32 on the Trading Economics EU50 table; MarketScreener put it at -1.28%. The EU50 was the weakest major gauge, reflecting its heavier weighting in the chip and industrial names at the centre of the selloff. Trading Economics still cites a June intraday all-time high of 6,357, so no record claim is made.
- **DAX** - a disclosed vendor discrepancy. The Trading Economics CFD-tracked DE40 closed 24,893.58, down 246.11 points (-0.98%) from a previous settle of 25,139.69, and the Trading Economics news item independently states the DAX "decreased 246 points or 0.98 percent ... to close at 24894 points." The Reuters European cash-close wrap, however, put the DAX at only -0.3%. The gap is a basis difference rather than a data error: Reuters cites the 17:30 CET Xetra cash close, while the Trading Economics CFD continued lower into the post-cash session as the US technology selloff deepened. The direction (down) is high confidence; the magnitude is bracketed by the two vendor figures and the precise CFD close is reported with the cash-close alternative disclosed alongside.
- **CAC 40** - 8,340.71, down 59.40 points (-0.71%) on the Trading Economics FR40 table (wrap: "fell 0.7% to close at 8,341"), its third straight daily decline. Reuters independently reported the CAC 40 down 0.7%.
- **FTSE 100** - the one precise official close: 10,428.85, down 9.00 points (-0.09%) from Monday's 10,437.85 on the Investors Chronicle/LSEG historical daily row (data provided by LSEG, delayed at least 15 minutes). Reuters reported the FTSE 100 at -0.2%; either way it was the standout relative outperformer.

## Session Drivers
The day was a broad risk-off move with two reinforcing engines. First, a global technology selloff: per Reuters, concerns over corporate AI spending and stretched valuations made tech the biggest weight on the STOXX 600, which logged a 3.7% sector drop - its biggest daily decline since February. Trading Economics tied the specific chip-led leg to fresh supply signals - SpaceX raising more cash after its IPO and Korea's SK Hynix signalling it would slow AI-chip production to allocate capacity to commodity DRAM. Second, rising rate-hike expectations: markets moved to price roughly 50 basis points of US Federal Reserve hikes by year-end to contain inflation, plus an additional 25 basis points from the European Central Bank later this year. A US Treasury 60-day license authorizing the sale of Iranian oil pushed crude to a three-month low, and euro-area June flash PMI confirmed a third straight month of private-sector contraction, the steepest in Germany since 2024.

## Sector Rotation
The tape was a clean defensives-over-cyclicals rotation. On the laggard side, technology (-3.7%) and mining (-3.3%, tracking lower precious-metal prices) led the STOXX 600 lower, per Reuters, and industrials with datacenter exposure were hit hard on Trading Economics data: Schneider Electric -4.53%, Siemens Energy -3.93%, Hochtief -4.3% and Siemens -2.42%. On the leader side, defensives outperformed: healthcare rose 1.9% and food and beverage 1.7%, with Sanofi up about 1.5% after the EU approved its Cenrifki treatment. Sector and single-stock percentages are approximate and carried at medium confidence; the index-level closes are high confidence.

## Standout Movers
Chipmakers were the day's epicentre. STMicroelectronics fell about 8.1% to 8.5% (Trading Economics France -8.1%; Reuters -8.5%), Infineon dropped 6.26% to 80.56, and ASML lost 5.74%. The damage spread to datacenter- and capex-exposed industrials: Schneider Electric -4.53%, Hochtief -4.3%, Siemens Energy -3.93% and Siemens -2.42%. Vonovia fell 3.21% after announcing a convertible-bond issue. The gainers were defensives and aerospace: Airbus +1.97% (intraday wrap +2.2%), SAP +1.74% (the Trading Economics news item logged +2.13%), Qiagen +1.51% and Sanofi +1.35%. Luxury was mixed and far calmer than the prior session, with LVMH up 0.49% while Hermes (-0.93%), L'Oreal (-0.56%) and Kering (-0.9%) eased. Single-stock percentages are carried at medium confidence.
