# Market Intelligence Pulse - 2026-06-23

## Summary

June 23 was a defensive cross-asset rotation led by the same AI and semiconductor complex that had carried risk assets into the prior rally. U.S. equities fell with the S&P 500 down 107.33 points, or 1.4%, to 7,365.46; the Nasdaq Composite down 579.56 points, or 2.2%, to 25,587.04; the Dow down only 45.87 points, or 0.1%, to 51,666.84; and the Russell 2000 down 28.92 points, or about 1%, to 2,975.48. [C-2026-06-23-US-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-23-SENTIMENT-C1-US-CLOSE]

The pressure started in Asia. South Korea's KOSPI fell 910.71 points, or 9.99%, to 8,203.84 and triggered a 20-minute market-wide halt after dropping more than 8%; Samsung Electronics fell 12.31% and SK Hynix fell 12.47%. Japan also sold off, with the Nikkei down 2,565.58 points to 69,788.38 and TOPIX down 104.67 points to 3,990.38. [C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-KOSPI-CLOSE-HALT, C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-KOREA-CHIPMAKERS, C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-JAPAN-CLOSE]

The macro tape did not give a simple recession signal. Treasury yields bull-steepened from the front end, with 2Y down 8 bp to 4.16%, 10Y down 1 bp to 4.50%, and 2s10s wider at +34 bp. The dollar rose 0.38% to 101.39, its highest since May 2025, as FedWatch hike odds jumped; Brent settled down 1.1% at $77.08 and WTI down 0.9% at $73.21 as Hormuz supply-route risk eased. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-DOLLAR, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-OIL-SETTLEMENT, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-SYNTHESIS]

Europe tracked the same risk-off theme. The STOXX Europe 600 closed at 634.64, down 0.72%; the EURO STOXX 50 fell 1.25%; the CAC 40 dropped 0.71%; and the FTSE 100 was the relative outperformer, down only 9.00 points, or 0.09%, to 10,428.85. Technology fell 3.7% and mining 3.3%, while healthcare rose 1.9% and food and beverage rose 1.7%. [C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE, C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-FTSE-DETAIL, C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-SECTOR-ROTATION]

Sentiment confirmed the damage. Fear & Greed dropped to 28, VIX rose 2.21 points, or 12.79%, to 19.49, and the volatility curve stayed upward sloping but with near-term stress close to the 30-day VIX. The latest AAII and flow readings had not yet refreshed, so they describe positioning before the selloff rather than a real-time response. ## Cross-Segment Read

The cleanest inference from the accepted evidence is that the AI and semiconductor trade became the market's pressure point. Asia supplied the first shock through Korea's near-10% KOSPI fall and Japan's large point losses; Europe carried the move through a 3.7% technology-sector drop; the U.S. confirmed it with the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index down 7.9%, the S&P 500 IT sector down 3.7%, and the Nasdaq down 2.21% in the Reuters closing wrap. [C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-KOSPI-CLOSE-HALT, C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-AI-TECH-DRIVER, C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-SECTOR-ROTATION, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-EQUITY-CLOSE]

The day was not a one-factor panic. Oil fell as Hormuz flows resumed and peace-talk progress reduced supply-route stress, which should have eased the inflation impulse, but the dollar still reached a 13-month high on hawkish Fed repricing and safe-haven demand. That combination made cheaper crude an offset rather than the dominant market signal. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-OIL-SETTLEMENT, C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-OIL-RELIEF, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-DOLLAR, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-SYNTHESIS]

Breadth and rotation kept the selloff from reading as full capitulation. The Dow lost only 0.1%, roughly 299 S&P 500 constituents traded higher despite the index decline, six of eleven S&P sectors rose in the Reuters macro wrap, and European healthcare plus food and beverage gained. Those facts are evidence of defensive rotation, not evidence that the risk-off move was harmless. [C-2026-06-23-US-C1-BREADTH-ROTATION, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-SECTOR-ROTATION, C-2026-06-23-SENTIMENT-C1-SYNTHESIS]

The sentiment layer is the confirmation point. Fear & Greed at 28 and a nearly 13% VIX rise show deeper fear, while AAII and flow readings still predate the selloff. The open question for the next report is whether fresh survey and flow data confirm a positioning break or show a one-day washout.
