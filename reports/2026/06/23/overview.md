# Global Markets - 2026-06-23

## So what

June 23 was a defensive rotation triggered by a crowded AI and semiconductor unwind. The U.S. S&P 500 fell 1.4% to 7,365.46 and the Nasdaq dropped 2.2% to 25,587.04, while the Dow lost only 0.1% because of lighter technology exposure. [C-2026-06-23-US-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-23-US-C1-BREADTH-ROTATION]

Asia showed the break first. South Korea's KOSPI fell 9.99% to 8,203.84 and triggered a 20-minute market-wide halt; Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix each fell more than 12%. Japan's Nikkei and TOPIX also sold off. [C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-KOSPI-CLOSE-HALT, C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-KOREA-CHIPMAKERS, C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-JAPAN-CLOSE]

The macro offset was real but secondary. Brent settled at $77.08, down 1.1%, and WTI at $73.21, down 0.9%, as Hormuz supply-route risk eased. But DXY rose to 101.39, FedWatch hike odds jumped, and the Treasury curve bull-steepened from the front end rather than rallying in a clean recession trade. [C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-OIL-SETTLEMENT, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-DOLLAR, C-2026-06-23-MACRO-C3-TREASURY-CURVE]

Europe confirmed the same pressure. The STOXX Europe 600 lost 0.72%, the EURO STOXX 50 fell 1.25%, and technology dropped 3.7%, while healthcare and food and beverage rose. The FTSE 100 was the relative outperformer at only -0.09%. [C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE, C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-SECTOR-ROTATION]

Sentiment moved into deeper fear. Fear & Greed fell to 28, while the latest AAII and flow readings still describe pre-selloff positioning rather than a real-time response. The next confirmation test is whether fresh survey or fund-flow data validate the price shock. [C-2026-06-23-SENTIMENT-C1-FEAR-GREED, C-2026-06-23-SENTIMENT-C1-AAII, C-2026-06-23-SENTIMENT-C1-FLOWS]

## Cross-Segment Read

The accepted evidence points to one main stress channel: crowded technology leadership unwound across regions. Korea supplied the most severe move, Europe showed the same pressure in technology and datacenter-exposed industrials, and the U.S. close showed semiconductor losses deep enough to pull the broader indexes lower. [C-2026-06-23-ASIA-C2-KOSPI-CLOSE-HALT, C-2026-06-23-EU-C1-MOVERS, C-2026-06-23-US-C1-CHIP-SELLOFF]

The counterweight was rotation. Lower oil, front-end Treasury bull-steepening, the Dow's small loss, and European defensive-sector gains softened the blow, but they did not change the headline regime from risk-off to risk-on.
