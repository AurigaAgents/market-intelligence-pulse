# Global Markets — June 1, 2026

## So what

Markets started June by refusing to panic. Oil jumped roughly 3% on fresh US-Iran strike and Hormuz-risk headlines, US futures still pointed higher, Japan and Korea closed at records, and VIX stayed in the mid-15s. That is the day's message: investors are treating geopolitics as an energy tax, not a systemic shock. The problem is that the same tape is also stretched. Rate markets are flirting with a Fed-hike narrative, Europe is waiting on an inflation print, Asia's rally is concentrated in AI hardware, and sentiment has already moved into greed. Risk-on remains the base case, but the margin of safety narrowed.

## Cross-segment synthesis

The common thread is a market separating price shock from crisis shock. Brent near $94 and WTI near $90 say traders are paying for a higher probability of supply disruption after the latest US-Iran headlines. Yet gold slipped toward $4,491, DXY stayed around 99, and equity volatility did not reprice. In a genuine geopolitical panic, oil, gold, the dollar, and VIX would usually rise together. Today, only oil clearly did. That makes energy inflation the transmission channel, not fear itself.

US assets show the tension most clearly. Futures pointed to a firmer open after a May rally that left the S&P 500 at 7,580.06 and VIX near four-month lows. Nvidia's Computex news added a fresh micro catalyst for the AI trade, helping semis and hardware partners even as Intel and AMD lagged. But the bond market is less forgiving: the 10-year yield at 4.47% and chatter that the next Fed move could be up place Friday's payrolls at the center of the week. If NFP validates the hike narrative, stretched equity multiples lose their easiest support.

Europe is quieter but not safer. The STOXX 600 slipped only 0.21%, with DAX, CAC, and FTSE moves small enough to call it positioning rather than capitulation. The decisive event is the May Eurozone HICP flash. April headline inflation accelerated to 3.0%, and country-level signals leave the market braced for stickiness. The oddity is that Bund yields are falling, with the German 10-year around 2.93%. Stocks are cautious because inflation may stay hot; bonds are cautious because growth may not. That split limits conviction.

Asia confirms that the global equity bid is selective. Japan and South Korea closed at records, with the Nikkei at 66,934.33 and KOSPI at 8,788.38, driven by AI hardware, SoftBank/Samsung exposure, and Nvidia-linked expectations. Hang Seng participated modestly, but Shanghai fell 0.27%. The regional signal is not “Asia is broadly strong.” It is “AI supply chains are being repriced while mainland China remains a relative drag.” Oil and possible BOJ tightening add a cap to how far Japan/Korea multiples can expand without earnings support.

Sentiment is supportive but no longer cheap. CNN Fear & Greed at 60, a VIX in the high-15s, and an overbought S&P 500 all point to a market comfortable taking risk. The corrected historical note matters: the nine-week S&P winning streak is rare, but not as rare as one analyst line implied. Still, the combination of Greed, low savings at 2.6%, and rate-sensitive leadership means downside can travel quickly if the macro data turn.

Base case: stay constructive while AI leadership holds, oil stays below the mid-$90s, and bond yields do not break higher. The failure mode is precise: an Iran headline or hot payroll print forces oil and rates up together, exposing how much of the rally rests on low volatility.
