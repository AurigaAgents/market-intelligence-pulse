# Market Intelligence Pulse - 2026-06-26

## Summary

June 26 was a global AI and semiconductor unwind with a partial relief bid underneath it. The U.S. close looked mild at the headline level: the S&P 500 slipped less than 0.1% to 7,354.02, the Dow fell 0.1% to 51,876.11, the Nasdaq lost 0.2% to 25,297.62, and the Russell 2000 rose 0.1% to 3,010.08. The week was harsher for large-cap technology, with the Nasdaq down 4.6%, but AP reported that nearly two out of every three S&P 500 stocks rose, so the day was concentrated weakness rather than broad liquidation. [C-2026-06-26-US-C1-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-26-US-C1-BREADTH]

The common equity drag was the AI hardware complex. In the U.S., AP identified Micron's 6.7% drop as the heaviest market weight and reported ON Semiconductor down 23.7% after its Synaptics acquisition agreement. In Asia, Trading Economics reported SoftBank Group down 12.19%, Advantest down 10.67%, Samsung Electronics down 6.28%, SK Hynix down 8.95%, and SMIC down 6.8%. Europe echoed the move through pressure on Infineon, ASML, STMicroelectronics, Siemens Energy, banks, autos, retail, energy and chemicals. [C-2026-06-26-US-C1-AI-CHIP-DRAG, C-2026-06-26-AS-C2-TECH-SELLOFF, C-2026-06-26-EU-C1-SECTOR-ROTATION, C-2026-06-26-EU-C1-MOVERS]

Oil and rates provided the counterweight. AP reported Brent crude down 3.8% to $72.60 and the 10-year Treasury yield down to 4.37% from 4.40% late Thursday; Reuters reported Brent futures down 4.34% to settle at about $72/bbl, spot gold up 1.06% to $4,068.72/oz, and the dollar index down 0.16% to 101.35. The official Treasury curve also eased at the 2-year, 5-year and 10-year points, while the 30-year rose 1 bp. [C-2026-06-26-US-C1-OIL-RATES, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-OIL-FX-GOLD, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-TREASURY-CURVE]

The macro problem did not go away. BEA reported May PCE inflation at +0.4% month over month and +4.1% year over year, with core PCE at +0.3% month over month and +3.4% year over year. CNBC reported that Minneapolis Fed President Neel Kashkari moved from expecting one rate cut by year-end to one rate hike by year-end, citing supply dynamics including tariffs, fertilizer disruption, energy and oil prices around the Strait of Hormuz, and data-center investment. [C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-PCE-INFLATION, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-FED-POLICY-RISK]

Sentiment was split in the same way as prices. VIX fell 2.54% to 18.41 and the Cboe total put/call ratio dropped below 1.0 for the first time since June 12, but CNN's Fear & Greed Index slipped to 25, with stock-price breadth and the put/call sub-indicator in extreme-fear territory. The accepted sentiment synthesis calls the regime "spot vol easing while composite fear deepens." ## Cross-Segment Read

The strongest signal was not simply "technology down"; it was an AI-infrastructure repricing that crossed regions. The U.S. damage came through Micron and ON Semiconductor, Asia through SoftBank, Advantest, Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix and SMIC, and Europe through Infineon, ASML, STMicroelectronics, Siemens Energy and AI-exposed cyclicals. Reuters' global risk-tone claim captures the aggregate: MSCI world down 0.53%, MSCI Asia ex-Japan down nearly 3%, European technology down 1.17%, and chip stocks down 5.3% on the day and 7.7% for the week. [C-2026-06-26-US-C1-AI-CHIP-DRAG, C-2026-06-26-AS-C2-TECH-SELLOFF, C-2026-06-26-EU-C1-MOVERS, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-GLOBAL-RISK-TONE]

The relief channel was real but incomplete. Lower Brent, lower intermediate Treasury yields, weaker dollar index and lower consumer inflation expectations helped stabilize U.S. breadth and let Australia outperform through miners. The same oil move also hurt European energy shares, so lower crude was an inflation-relief signal and a sector headwind at the same time. [C-2026-06-26-US-C1-OIL-RATES, C-2026-06-26-AS-C2-AUSTRALIA, C-2026-06-26-AS-C2-CROSS-ASSET, C-2026-06-26-EU-C1-CROSS-ASSET, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-CONSUMER-SENTIMENT]

The macro and sentiment layers argue against calling the session clean risk-on. PCE inflation stayed elevated, Kashkari's rate outlook shifted hawkish, the yen remained near a 40-year low, gold rose, Fear & Greed fell to 25, and index put/call ratios still showed defensive hedging. The better phrase is late-week relief inside a defensive global tape. [C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-PCE-INFLATION, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-FED-POLICY-RISK, C-2026-06-26-AS-C2-YEN-FX-MACRO, C-2026-06-26-MACRO-C2-OIL-FX-GOLD, C-2026-06-26-SENTIMENT-C1-SYNTHESIS]
