# Global Markets - 2026-06-26

## So what

June 26 was an AI and semiconductor unwind, not a broad liquidation. The U.S. headline tape was only slightly lower, and nearly two out of every three S&P 500 stocks rose, but Micron and ON Semiconductor dragged the cap-weighted indexes while the Nasdaq finished a 4.6% weekly decline. [1-4]

The same pressure was global. Asia sold off hard in Japan and South Korea as AI and hardware names reversed, while Europe gave back part of Thursday's record STOXX 600 close as AI-infrastructure, chips, banks and autos weakened. [5-19]

The offset was lower oil and easier intermediate yields. Brent moved down toward about $72/bbl, the 10-year Treasury eased, the dollar index slipped, and spot gold rose. That helped U.S. breadth and Australia's miner-led outperformance, but it did not erase the inflation and Fed overhang. [2-4, 9-10, 12, 20-22]

Sentiment captured the contradiction: VIX fell to 18.41 and total put/call dropped below 1.0, yet CNN Fear & Greed slipped to 25 and index hedging stayed visible. The accepted sentiment read is "spot vol easing while composite fear deepens." [2, 23-31]

## Watch Points

- AI/chip selling: whether the Japan/Korea-style unwind spreads into broader U.S. breadth. [1-2, 5-7, 11-13]
- Policy risk: whether sticky PCE and Kashkari's rate-hike signal outweigh lower oil and yields. [12, 21-22, 32]
- Sentiment divergence: whether lower spot volatility or deeper composite fear becomes the next session's dominant signal. [2, 23, 25, 30-31]
