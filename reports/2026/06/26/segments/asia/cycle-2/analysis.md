## Overview
Asia-Pacific equities sold off sharply on Friday, June 26, 2026, as traders took profits after AI- and chip-linked rallies had pushed Japan and South Korea to record highs earlier in the week. AP described the regional session as "shares plunged Friday in Asia, led by heavy losses in Japan and South Korea as traders sold to lock in gains from recent rallies in stocks related to artificial intelligence." Reuters reported that MSCI's Asia ex-Japan index fell nearly 3%, while South Korea's KOSPI lost as much as 5.8%.

The session was strongest in one narrow place: Australia. The ASX 200 closed up on a cash-market basis at 8,764.20, +15.50 points / +0.18% from a prior close of 8,748.70, using Yahoo Finance quote and historical data. Trading Economics reported the same direction in its news stream, saying the ASX 200 edged up 16 points, or 0.2%, to close at 8,764, while its separate CFD table showed 8,765.80 / +0.20%. This cycle uses the Yahoo cash-close basis for the ASX point-change math and does not mix it with the Trading Economics CFD basis.

Outside Australia, the tape was risk-off. Trading Economics reported the KOSPI at 8,411.21, down 519.09 points / -5.81%, led lower by Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix, SK Square, Hyundai Motor, LG Energy Solution, Hanwha Aerospace, Doosan Enerbility, and HD Hyundai Heavy Industries. The Hang Seng fell 405.05 points / -1.76% to 22,671.86. Xinhua reported the Shanghai Composite down 2.26% to 4,027.26, the Shenzhen Component down 3.44% to 15,782.22, the ChiNext down 4.07% to 4,194.21, and the STAR Composite down 2.02% to 2,343.66.

The macro backdrop did not explain the selloff by itself. Reuters reported Brent crude settling down 4.34% at about $72 per barrel as more tankers left the Strait of Hormuz, the US 10-year yield falling to 4.38%, and spot gold rising 1.06% to $4,068.72. Those cross-asset moves were supportive for oil-import-dependent Asia and for Australian gold miners, but they were overwhelmed in Japan and Korea by the AI hardware unwind and by concern that Apple price hikes were signaling structural inflation from scarce memory and other tech inputs.

## Session Status
Friday, June 26, 2026 was treated as a regular cash-equity trading session for the Tokyo, Seoul, Hong Kong, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and Australian markets referenced in this segment. AP, Reuters, Xinhua, Trading Economics, Yahoo Finance, and XTB all carried same-day market coverage for the session. The core market driver was profit-taking after the earlier AI and memory-chip rally, with Japan and South Korea hit hardest and Australia diverging positively.

## AI and Technology Reversal
Technology and semiconductor-linked stocks drove the selloff. Trading Economics reported that Japan's losses were led by SoftBank Group (-12.19%), Taiyo Yuden (-11.48%), and Advantest (-10.67%); its component table also showed Tokyo Electron -4.26%. In South Korea, Samsung Electronics fell 6.28%, SK Hynix fell 8.95%, and SK Square fell 9.43%, with Hyundai Motor, LG Energy Solution, Hanwha Aerospace, Doosan Enerbility, and HD Hyundai Heavy Industries also sharply lower.

AP framed the move as profit-taking after AI-related rallies. Reuters added that Apple's price hikes had fueled worries about structural inflation from massive AI spending and limited availability of key tech components. XTB's morning wrap corroborated the Asia open and intraday pressure, calling the move a broad hardware and AI unwind after Apple and Microsoft price hikes.

## Hong Kong
Hong Kong's Hang Seng fell 405.05 points / -1.76% to 22,671.86. Trading Economics said the decline extended losses as a broad selloff in artificial intelligence and semiconductor-related stocks weighed on sentiment across regional markets. Technology, financial, and producer manufacturing shares came under pressure. Tencent fell 2.28%, AIA 1.94%, Xiaomi 3.95%, Meituan 2.80%, and SMIC 6.8%. Lingyi iTech's HK$8.3 billion Hong Kong IPO underscored continuing interest in AI-related listings but did not improve broader sentiment.

## Mainland China
Mainland Chinese benchmarks closed sharply lower in Xinhua's official close summary. The Shanghai Composite fell 2.26% to 4,027.26, the Shenzhen Component fell 3.44% to 15,782.22, the ChiNext lost 4.07% to 4,194.21, and the STAR Composite closed 2.02% lower at 2,343.66. Turnover across Shanghai and Shenzhen was 3.55 trillion yuan, or about $521 billion, down from 3.59 trillion yuan in the previous session. Education and pork shares led gains, while cobalt metal and battery-industry stocks posted sharp declines.

## Australia
Australia was the regional exception. On the Yahoo cash-market basis, the ASX 200 closed at 8,764.20, up 15.50 points / +0.18% from a previous close of 8,748.70. Trading Economics' news stream rounded the same direction to a 16-point / 0.2% gain and said process industries, retail trade, and transport shares supported the index. Rising copper and gold prices helped miners: BHP gained 0.8%, Rio Tinto 2.2%, and gold producers Northern Star and Evolution Mining each rose about 3%. Three of the big four banks rose modestly, while CBA slipped 0.42% and CSL fell 2.36%.

The local resilience was not a clean risk-on signal. Trading Economics also reported that the ASX 200 fell 0.7% for the week, reversing strength in the prior two sessions, as resilient domestic data kept concern alive that the RBA may tighten further. Traders were also cautious ahead of China PMI data and the RBA's June meeting minutes.

## Yen, Rates, and FX
Reuters reported that the yen was near its weakest level against the dollar in 40 years at 161.76, beyond the 160 level seen by many as a line in the sand for Japanese authorities. The dollar index fell 0.16% to 101.35 but was headed for a second straight weekly gain. The euro was up 0.14% at $1.1385 but was on track for a second consecutive weekly loss versus the dollar. The US 10-year yield fell 1.16 bp to 4.38%, and the 2-year yield fell 2.48 bp to 4.096%.

Tokyo inflation added a second macro cross-current for Japan. Trading Economics and XTB reported that Tokyo core CPI accelerated in June, reinforcing expectations that the Bank of Japan could continue raising interest rates. The weak yen supports exporter translations, but the combination of intervention risk and rising-rate expectations added fragility to a session already dominated by AI profit-taking.

## Cross-Asset Context
Reuters reported that Brent crude futures fell 4.34% to settle at about $72 per barrel as crude prices tumbled on easing supply concerns and more oil tankers left the Strait of Hormuz. Reuters also reported that Saudi Aramco resumed oil loading at Ras Tanura after a nearly four-month halt, while noting that a cargo vessel was hit near Oman. Lower oil would normally support oil-importing Asia, but the equity selloff overrode that tailwind in Japan and Korea.

Gold moved in the opposite direction: Reuters reported spot gold up 1.06% to $4,068.72 per ounce. That helped explain Australia's relative outperformance because Trading Economics reported that gold producers Northern Star and Evolution Mining rose about 3% each and that rising gold and copper supported miners.
