## Overview

US cash-equity sentiment on Friday, June 26, 2026 is best described as **spot vol easing while composite fear deepens — a bifurcated sentiment regime**. The session delivered a fifth straight Nasdaq decline, but the S&P 500 slipped less than 0.1% and the Russell 2000 rose 0.1%. More importantly, nearly two out of every three S&P 500 stocks rose, confirming that the selling remained concentrated in large AI-linked names rather than spreading broadly. AP identified Micron Technology's 6.7% decline as the heaviest weight on the market and reported ON Semiconductor dropped 23.7% after its Synaptics acquisition agreement.

The sentiment stack tells a bifurcated story. VIX dropped 2.54% to 18.41, with the intraday range (18.20–20.72) showing that the opening AI-stock panic was priced out through the afternoon as Brent crude fell 3.8% to $72.60 and the 10-year Treasury yield eased to 4.37%. The Cboe total put/call ratio fell below 1.0 for the first time since June 12, and the ETP put/call ratio dropped sharply from 1.38 to 1.14. But the Fear & Greed Index slipped further to 25 (fear), with CNN's headline text reading "extreme fear is driving the US market." The put/call sub-indicator deepened from "fear" to "extreme fear" (5-day average 0.69), and stock price breadth remained in "extreme fear" territory (McClellan Volume Summation Index at 1,408.51). The divergence between declining spot volatility and deepening composite fear is the defining signal of this cycle.

The macro backdrop offered late-week relief. AP reported Brent crude down 3.8% to $72.60, below its level before the US and Israel attacked Iran, the 10-year Treasury yield eased to 4.37% from 4.40% late Thursday, and one-year consumer inflation expectations edged down to 4.6% from 4.8% in May. But the Fed backdrop stayed hawkish: CNBC reported Minneapolis Fed President Neel Kashkari shifted from one cut to one hike by year-end, with May headline PCE at 4.1% and core PCE at 3.4%.

The regime is not a clean risk-on (composite sentiment is at 25, Fed is hawkish, inflation is above 4%) and not the June 23 panic (VIX fell, oil fell, breadth was positive at the stock level). It is a late-week relief bid layered over a structurally cautious positioning environment, with the institutional tail-hedge bid in index options persisting even as single-stock options flow stays bullish.

## Session Status
Friday, June 26, 2026 was a regular cash-equity and derivatives trading day across all referenced US venues, with no market holidays. The session capped a volatile week in which the Nasdaq Composite booked its fifth straight decline, the S&P 500 fell 2.0% week-over-week, and the Dow and Russell 2000 finished the week higher. Two cross-cutting forces shaped the Friday tape: persistent AI-linked and semiconductor selling (Micron -6.7%, ON Semiconductor -23.7%, SoftBank -12.5% in Japan) and easing macro headwinds (Brent crude -3.8%, 10-year yield down to 4.37%, consumer inflation expectations easing). The net result was a narrow cap-weighted decline with strong internal breadth — nearly two out of every three S&P 500 stocks rose — and a sharp drop in spot volatility.

## Fear & Greed Index
The Fear & Greed Index deepened in fear territory on June 26. CNN's page displayed 25 (fear) with the headline text "extreme fear is driving the US market," "Previous close 25," "1 week ago 37," "1 month ago 60," "1 year ago 63," and "Last updated Jun 26 at 5:23:14 PM ET." Finhacker.cz's CNN-tracker showed 26 (fear) with a data-updated timestamp of "Jun 24, 2026, 8:00 PM ET." The one-point CNN–Finhacker gap (25 vs 26) is a freshness artifact, not a methodological disagreement. The composite slipped one point from June 25's 26 to June 26's 25 on CNN, and is down 12 points from one month ago.

Four of the five visible CNN sub-indicators were labeled fear or extreme fear at the time of access: market momentum (fear), stock price strength (fear, NYSE net new 52-week highs/lows 1.47%), stock price breadth (extreme fear, McClellan Volume Summation Index at 1,408.51), and put/call options (extreme fear, 5-day average 0.69). Only market volatility was neutral (VIX below its 50-day moving average). The deepening of the put/call sub-indicator from "fear" on June 25 to "extreme fear" on June 26 is the notable day-over-day shift, consistent with the fifth consecutive Nasdaq decline and the persistent AI-stock overhang. The CNN "Index Update" banner notes the ongoing McClellan Summation Index methodology change for the Stock Price Breadth indicator.

## Volatility
Yahoo Finance historical data showed VIX closing at 18.41 on June 26, down 0.48 points or 2.54% from 18.89 on June 25, with OHLC of open 19.70, high 20.72, low 18.20, close 18.41. Google Finance, MarketWatch, and the Wall Street Journal all corroborate the 18.41 close. The intraday pattern is notable: VIX opened higher at 19.70, spiked to 20.72 during the morning AI-stock selloff, then steadily eased through the afternoon as Brent crude fell 3.8% and the 10-year Treasury yield dropped to 4.37%, closing near the session low.

The Cboe volatility curve remained in contango and upward sloping across all tenors: VIX at 18.41 (June 26 close), VIX9D at 17.92 (June 25 latest available), VIX3M at 20.33 (June 25 latest available), VIX6M at 22.35 (June 25 latest available), and VIX1Y at 23.50 (June 25 latest available). The VIX9D, VIX3M, VIX6M, and VIX1Y values for June 26 had not been populated on Yahoo Finance at collection time; the June 25 closes are reported as the latest available with an explicit freshness note. The VIX9D-to-VIX gap narrowed to approximately 0.49 points from June 25's 0.97-point spread using the latest available VIX9D; the true June 26 gap may differ if VIX9D also eased. The sharp spot-vol decline, despite the cap-weighted S&P 500's fifth straight decline, reflects the market's recognition that the selling remained concentrated in AI-linked mega-caps while breadth was improving and macro tail risks (oil, rates) eased.

## Options Positioning
Cboe end-of-day options statistics for June 26 showed a two-tier put/call pattern. The total put/call ratio was 0.99 — the first sub-1.0 reading since June 12 — with equity put/call at 0.67, index put/call at 1.18, exchange-traded products put/call at 1.14, VIX put/call at 0.57, and SPX+SPXW put/call at 1.23. Total options volume was 13,853,230 contracts (call 6,968,999 / put 6,884,231).

Compared to June 25 (total 1.02, equity 0.61, index 1.16, ETP 1.38, SPX 1.31), the total ratio dropped below 1.0, the ETP ratio fell sharply from 1.38 to 1.14, and the SPX ratio eased from 1.31 to 1.23. The equity ratio rose from 0.61 to 0.67, indicating slightly less bullish single-stock positioning. The pattern — bullish single-stock options flow (equity P/C 0.67, well below 1.0) alongside persistent defensive index hedging (index P/C 1.18, SPX P/C 1.23) — confirms that institutions continue buying tail protection via index products while retail positioning remains bullish on individual names. The CNN Fear & Greed 5-day average put/call ratio of 0.69 (extreme fear zone) is a different construction from the Cboe end-of-day ratios but points in the same direction: options-market sentiment is cautious overall, with the defensive bid concentrated in index products.

## AAII Sentiment Survey

The AAII survey captured the improvement in mood driven by the Iran de-escalation, the Micron-guidance-led chip rally, and the in-line May PCE release. ## Macro Backdrop
AP reported Brent crude down 3.8% to $72.60, below where it stood before the US and Israel attacked Iran, and the 10-year Treasury yield eased to 4.37% from 4.40% late Thursday. AP also cited a decline in one-year US consumer inflation expectations to 4.6% from 4.8% in May. The macro relief from falling oil and yields did not remove Fed risk: CNBC reported that Minneapolis Fed President Neel Kashkari changed his outlook from one rate cut by year-end to one rate hike by year-end, citing Middle East supply risk, tariffs, energy and fertilizer disruptions, and data-center investment pressure. CNBC also reported May headline PCE inflation at 4.1% (highest since April 2023) and core PCE at 3.4%, with inflation above the Fed's 2% goal for five years. The cross-asset price action — Brent down sharply, yields easing, consumer inflation expectations easing — is consistent with a late-week relief bid in risk assets, but the Fed backdrop remains hawkish and inflation is still above 4%, capping the upside for sentiment.

The Cboe total put/call ratio dropped below 1.0 for the first time since June 12, and the ETP and SPX put/call ratios both eased from June 25 levels. The AAII survey's 44.9% bullish print is now two days stale and captures the Iran-de-escalation and chip-rally optimism rather than the Friday action. The regime is not a clean risk-on (composite sentiment is at 25, Fed is hawkish, inflation is above 4%) and not the June 23 panic (VIX fell, oil fell, breadth was positive at the stock level).
