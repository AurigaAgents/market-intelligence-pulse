## Session Status
Saturday, June 20 is a no-session date for the covered Asia-Pacific cash-equity read. JPX states its markets are closed on Saturdays and Sundays. HKEX's live calendar notice says Hong Kong markets were closed on Friday, June 19 and re-open as usual on Monday, June 22. FRED's Nikkei 225 series has a June 19 observation and a next release date of June 22, not a June 20 update. The live market-data pages for the covered benchmarks list latest observations dated June 18 or June 19.

That calendar structure matters for interpretation. There is no single synchronized "Asia close" for June 20. The cleanest basis is a latest-verified snapshot: Friday closes for markets that traded on June 19, and Thursday closes for markets that were already in Dragon Boat Festival closure or whose latest available source observation was June 18.

## Standing Levels Into the Weekend
Japan carried the strongest live Friday signal. FRED records the Nikkei 225 at 71,250.06 on June 19 after 71,053.49 on June 18, a gain of 196.57 points or 0.28%. Trading Economics corroborates the 71,250 level and says technology shares mostly advanced while financial, industrial and consumer shares were generally weaker. This was a narrow positive rather than a broad regional risk-on print.

Greater China was not a Friday live-equity read. Hong Kong's HK50 last stood at 23,924.81 on June 18, down 387.35 points or 1.59%, and HKEX confirms Hong Kong markets were closed June 19 with a June 22 re-open. Mainland China's latest June 18 snapshot was split: the Shanghai Composite fell 17.59 points or 0.43% to 4,090.48, while CSI 300 rose 10.21 points or 0.21% to 4,941.60. Trading Economics states mainland markets would be closed June 19 for Dragon Boat Festival. Taiwan's latest listed observation was also June 18, with TSI up 587.81 points or 1.28% to 46,465.20.

India, Korea, Australia and Singapore were softer on June 19. India's SENSEX fell 607.07 points or 0.78% to 76,802.90, and NIFTY 50 lost 154.90 points or 0.64% to 24,013.10, though Trading Economics still described the index as up 1.7% for the week. KOSPI edged down 11.42 points or 0.13% to 9,052.42. Australia's ASX200 fell 82.40 points or 0.92% to 8,828.70. Singapore's STI declined 20.14 points or 0.39% to 5,192.70.

## Drivers
The regional driver was a split between semiconductor/AI resilience and renewed risk trimming. Japan and Taiwan supplied the positive side: Japan's tech shares mostly advanced, while Taiwan's latest listed TSI close was up 1.28% with Taiwan Semiconductor up 1.05% on the same source table. South Korea showed the more cautious version of the same theme: the KOSPI slipped after recent record-zone gains, with Trading Economics citing renewed Middle East uncertainty and profit-taking, but also noting foreign investors remained net buyers.

The negative pockets had local texture. Hong Kong's June 18 retreat was led by financial, tech and retail shares. Mainland China diverged internally, with big-bank weakness weighing on Shanghai Composite while CSI 300 still rose. India was pulled lower by large-cap IT and financial laggards even as Bharti Airtel and ICICI Bank were positive. Australia's slide was more materials- and industrials-led, with BHP pressured after flagging Jansen Stage 2 potash cost overruns and an impairment charge. Singapore's pullback was concentrated enough in banks to matter but still left the STI near its June 2026 record zone.
