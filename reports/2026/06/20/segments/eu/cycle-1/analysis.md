## Overview
There was no European regular trading session on the June 20, 2026 report date. June 20 is a Saturday, and European cash-equity venues trade only Monday through Friday: Euronext states its cash and derivatives markets are open Monday to Friday throughout 2026, and the London Stock Exchange operates its trading services on weekdays. So this is a no-session cycle. Unlike the US — where the immediately preceding Friday, June 19, was the Juneteenth holiday — European markets traded normally on June 19, because Juneteenth closes only US venues. The last European regular session was therefore Friday, June 19, 2026, one session more recent than the last US close. The honest European read for June 20 is the verified weekend closure plus the standing levels and week-in-review carried in from the June 19 close, not a new daily print.

## Session Status
Saturday, June 20 is a weekend day with no European cash-equity session. Euronext's 2026 trading-hours and holidays page states that "the Euronext Cash Markets and the Euronext Derivatives Markets will be open Monday to Friday throughout 2026" except on listed holidays, and the London Stock Exchange's business-days page states it "generally operates its Trading Services each weekday." The FTSE 100 historical daily table is itself confirmation: its most recent row is Friday, June 19, with no Saturday entry. There are no June 20 open, intraday, or close prints to report, and none are manufactured here.

A point of asymmetry with the US segment for the same report date: the last US regular session was Thursday, June 18, because Friday, June 19, was the Juneteenth holiday on US exchanges. European venues do not observe Juneteenth, so they traded a full session on Friday, June 19. The European standing levels are therefore one trading day more recent than the US ones.

## Last-Session Drivers (carried context)
The driver of the June 19 session was geopolitical rather than rate-led. European shares inched lower from record highs after scheduled US-Iran talks in Switzerland were called off as fresh fighting flared in Lebanon; Israel and Hezbollah later agreed a ceasefire. With US markets shut for Juneteenth, there was no live Wall Street session to track. Oil and gas stocks led gains while miners and travel lagged. Luxury fell (LVMH -1.2%, L'Oreal -1.9%, Hermes -2.3%) alongside tech (ASML -1%, Prosus -2.2%); Volkswagen dropped more than 4% as it traded ex-dividend, while German defense names (Rheinmetall +1.8%, Renk +1.5%, Hensoldt +1.5%) and Mercedes-Benz (+1.9%, on reported EU tariffs on Chinese plug-in hybrids) gained. In France, Airbus (-1.3%) and Safran (-1.2%) weakened while TotalEnergies added about 1%. On policy, the ECB's Pierre Wunsch said the bank could raise rates again as soon as next month if inflation broadened beyond energy. This is last-session context, not a report-date development.
