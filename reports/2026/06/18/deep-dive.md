# The Relief Rally That Still Priced a Hawkish Fed - 2026-06-18

## The hook

The important June 18 move was that risk assets rallied without erasing the policy shock. The S&P 500 rose to 7,500.58, the Nasdaq jumped to 26,517.93, the Russell 2000 outperformed at 2,979.77, and the VIX fell to 16.40. But the CNN Fear & Greed Index stayed in "Fear," and FedWatch still put the chance of a 25 bp September hike near 50%. ## Why it mattered

The rebound had two visible engines. First, geopolitical supply risk eased: AP described an initial U.S.-Iran agreement with a 60-day negotiating clock and oil-sale waivers, while the macro source trail said the Hormuz route normally handles about 20 million bpd of petroleum liquids and that the blockade on Iranian ports on the strait had been lifted. Second, chips led the equity tape: AP reported Intel, Nvidia, and Micron gains, and the PHLX Semiconductor Index rose 6.4% after the Apple-Intel partnership news. [C-2026-06-18-US-C2-US-IRAN-DEAL, C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-HORMUZ-SUPPLY, C-2026-06-18-US-C2-TECH-LEADERSHIP, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-007]

Rates helped, but only at the margin. The official Treasury par curve put the 2-year at 4.19%, the 10-year at 4.46%, and the 30-year at 4.90%, a modest bull-flattening from June 17. That eased funding-pressure optics for small caps, but it did not cancel the FOMC backdrop: the June SEP still had a 3.8% median end-2026 funds-rate projection, 9 of 18 participants above the current midpoint, and 2026 PCE/core PCE medians at 3.6% and 3.3%. [C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-004, C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-FED-SEP]

## The caveat

Breadth and geography kept the rally from becoming a clean global risk-on call. Only five of 11 S&P 500 sectors rose. Europe closed mixed-to-lower under rate concerns, with the STOXX 600 and FTSE 100 down even as some continental indexes rose. Asia had strong Japan and Korea records, but Shanghai and Hong Kong closed lower, and Korea's record KOSPI close came despite weak issue breadth. [C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-006, C-2026-06-18-EU-C3-RATE-CONCERNS, C-2026-06-18-EU-C3-STOXX600-CLOSE, C-2026-06-18-EU-C3-FTSE100-CLOSE, C-2026-06-18-ASIA-C2-REGIONAL-BREADTH, C-2026-06-18-ASIA-C2-KOSPI-CHIP-BREADTH]

## Our take

The base case is relief with a policy ceiling. The accepted evidence supports a real tail-risk reduction from U.S.-Iran/Hormuz developments and a real equity leadership impulse from chips. It does not support treating June 18 as a broad easing cycle: DXY rose to 100.72, FedWatch still priced meaningful September hike odds, and the sentiment gauge remained in fear territory. [C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-DXY, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-010, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-008, C-2026-06-18-US-C2-US-IRAN-DEAL, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-007]

**Conviction:** Medium-high. The source trail is strong on the equity rebound, chip leadership, lower VIX, and modest Treasury easing; it is weaker for any blanket "global risk-on" conclusion because breadth, Europe, China-linked Asia, and Fed pricing all stayed mixed.
