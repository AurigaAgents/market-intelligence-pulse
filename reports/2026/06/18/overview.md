# Global Markets - 2026-06-18

## So what

June 18 was a selective relief rally, not a clean all-clear. U.S. equities rebounded into the Juneteenth market holiday, with the S&P 500 up 1.1%, the Nasdaq up 1.9%, and the Russell 2000 up 2.1%; the VIX fell to 16.40, but CNN Fear & Greed was still only 38, in "Fear." The strongest positive channel was technology and chips. AP reported Intel up 10.6%, Nvidia up 3.0%, and Micron up 8.7%, while the sentiment segment put the PHLX Semiconductor Index up 6.4%. The catch is breadth: only five of 11 S&P 500 sectors rose. [C-2026-06-18-US-C2-TECH-LEADERSHIP, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-007, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-006]

Macro did not fully validate the equity bounce. The Treasury curve eased to 4.19% on the 2-year and 4.46% on the 10-year, but the June SEP still showed a 3.8% median end-2026 funds-rate projection, FedWatch still put September hike odds near 50%, and DXY rose to 100.72. [C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-FED-SEP, C-2026-06-18-SENT-C1-010, C-2026-06-18-MACRO-C2-DXY]

The regional picture stayed split. Europe was mixed-to-lower, with the STOXX 600 and FTSE 100 down even as DAX, CAC 40, and EURO STOXX 50 rose; Asia paired record Japan/Korea strength with lower Shanghai and Hong Kong closes.
