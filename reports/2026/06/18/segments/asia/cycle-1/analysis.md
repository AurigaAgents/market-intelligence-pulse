## Drivers
The regional signal is bipolar. The four tracked indexes split exactly two-up, two-down, and the gap between best and worst was the widest in recent sessions: KOSPI led at +2.25% while Hang Seng lagged at −1.59%, a 3.84-percentage-point spread.

Japan and South Korea both rallied firmly. The Nikkei 225's +1.65% close is directly supported by the official Nikkei Indexes page, which puts the index above 71,000 for the first time and shows the intraday high at 71,398.58 (marked at 10:14). Trading Economics attributes the KOSPI's +2.25% surge to continued semiconductor-led buying, naming SK Hynix, Samsung Electronics, SK Square, LG Energy Solution, and Hyundai Motor as contributors, and cites easing geopolitical tensions after the US-Iran memorandum of understanding as a broader risk-appetite support. This cycle does not add an unsourced catalyst to the Nikkei move.

Hong Kong's loss has a clear macro engine. Trading Economics reports the Fed struck a more hawkish tone, signaling further tightening may be needed; that lifted Treasury yields and the dollar, pressuring Asian sentiment overnight. The Hong Kong Monetary Authority mirrored the Fed by leaving its base rate unchanged at 4.0%. The Hang Seng's decline was broad-based but led by financial, technology, retail, and energy shares, with Tencent −1.2%, Meituan −3.5%, Xiaomi −3.3%, Lenovo −4.4%, and Pop Mart −4.1%. Mainland China's Shanghai Composite moved in the opposite direction to Hong Kong for the second session in a row, but only by a marginal −0.43%.

## Read-Through
For the full Market Pulse, the usable Asia message is divergence, not direction. Japan and Korea are rallying on semiconductor strength and improving risk sentiment; Hong Kong is selling off on US-rate expectations and a strong dollar. The Shanghai Composite's small decline keeps the mainland signal neutral-to-cautious rather than aligned with either the Korea/Japan rally or Hong Kong's sharper drop.

The Korea semiconductor narrative is now a multi-session theme: KOSPI has posted record or near-record closes across consecutive sessions, and Trading Economics names the same large-cap complex (SK Hynix, Samsung Electronics) as the engine. Hong Kong's three-session losing streak and lowest-since-July-2025 close mark the clearest bearish signal in the region and should be treated as the primary risk-off data point for the Asia segment.
