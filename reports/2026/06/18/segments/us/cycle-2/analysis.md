## Overview
US equities rebounded in the June 18, 2026 regular session and recovered most of the prior day's Fed-driven selloff. AP's closing index ledger showed the S&P 500 up 80.48 points, or 1.1%, to 7,500.58; the Dow up 72.15 points, or 0.1%, to 51,564.70; the Nasdaq Composite up 496.28 points, or 1.9%, to 26,517.93; and the Russell 2000 up 61.78 points, or 2.1%, to 2,979.77.

The session was risk-on, but not cleanly one-directional across assets. Treasury's official par curve showed the 10-year yield at 4.46% and the 2-year at 4.19% on June 18, both below the June 17 readings on the same basis. Oil was mixed rather than uniformly lower: AP reported Brent settling 0.4% higher at $79.85 while US benchmark crude fell 0.2% to $75.85. The geopolitical source trail supports an initial US-Iran agreement with a 60-day negotiating clock and oil-sanctions waivers; it does not support the blocked cycle-1 "14-point" formulation.

## Drivers
The rebound had three defensible drivers. First, rates gave equities room to recover: the Treasury curve eased on a consistent official basis, with the 2-year down 1 bp and the 10-year down 3 bp versus June 17. That supports AP's broader "Treasury yields eased" wording without mixing Trading Economics, AP late-day, and official Treasury bases.

Second, the energy channel became less inflationary without becoming a simple oil-down story. AP reported that oil prices wavered after the US and Iran signed an agreement to end the war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz to oil tanker traffic. Brent finished higher on the day while US benchmark crude finished lower, so the clean claim is "mixed oil, lower geopolitical tail risk," not "crude fell across the board."

Third, technology led. AP's global-market wrap said technology had some of the biggest gains and noted Intel +10.6%, Nvidia +3.0%, and Micron +8.7%. That helps explain why the Nasdaq outperformed the Dow, and why small caps could rally alongside lower yields even though the FOMC backdrop stayed hawkish.

## Read-Through
Cycle 2 replaces the blocked cycle-1 US narrative with a narrower, directly sourced account. The correct market read is a recovery session into a long holiday weekend: all four tracked US equity indexes were positive on the day, positive week to date, and positive year to date through the June 18 close. The Nasdaq's 2.4% week-to-date gain and Russell 2000's 2.1% daily gain show risk appetite improved, but the Treasury and oil ledgers argue against calling the macro signal unambiguously dovish.

The Fed source supports policy risk as context, not a precise causal chain. The June 17 statement held the target range at 3.50%-3.75% and explicitly referenced elevated uncertainty from the Middle East conflict and energy-related supply shocks. AP's market stories then reported that investors were still digesting the likelihood of a rate hike this year. That is enough to describe a post-FOMC rebound in risk assets, but not enough to claim the Fed directly attributed any dot-plot shift to the US-Iran conflict.
