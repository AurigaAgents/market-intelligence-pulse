## Analysis

European equities lost their five-day momentum on Thursday, June 18, 2026, with the pan-European **STOXX Europe 600** closing about **0.3% lower** (~637 from a prior 639.31) and snapping a five-session winning streak. The headline decline masked a sharply two-speed market: the large-cap German and French indices ground higher while the UK and the broad benchmark fell, a split driven by sector composition rather than any single macro shock. The two cross-currents of the day were an oil-driven relief impulse from the US-Iran interim deal pulling continental risk assets up, and a hawkish-Fed plus weak-commodity impulse pulling energy, mining, healthcare and the UK down.

The continental tape was firm. **Germany's DAX 40** rose **0.42% to 25,038.80**, crossing 25,000 for the first time since early June and notching a sixth straight gain, as semiconductors and energy-sensitive industrials led — **Infineon +6.0%**, **Siemens Energy +4.7%**, Siemens +1.3% and Airbus. **France's CAC 40** added **0.44% to 8,467.98**, rebounding from the prior session with **Safran +2.9%**, **Schneider Electric +2.9%**, **Airbus +2.6%** and **STMicroelectronics +4.2%** (tracking US chip strength after its $1.5bn convertible sale). The blue-chip **Euro STOXX 50** finished **near flat (+0.06%, ~6,311)**, holding just shy of its June record of 6,345.40. The progress toward ending the Iran war — an interim pact extending the April ceasefire by 60 days and fully reopening the Strait of Hormuz to shipping — pushed oil to its lowest since the first trading day of the war and outweighed the hawkish Fed for these markets.

The offsets were concentrated and heavy. **Britain's FTSE 100 fell 1.04% to 10,399.70**, the weakest major index, weighed by its energy and mining tilt as commodities slid: Shell -2.4%, BP -2.9%, Rio Tinto -3.0%, with the **mining subsector down 3.1%** — the day's worst — against a firmer dollar. **Healthcare**, a pan-European heavyweight bloc, was the quiet driver of the STOXX 600's decline: **AstraZeneca -2.4%, Roche -2.7%, Novartis -2.5% and Sanofi -2.1%**. **Automakers** sat at the bottom alongside mining, with Mercedes-Benz, Volkswagen and Stellantis down 2.8%-4.6% and **BMW -4%**, extending the prior session's 8.3% profit-warning slide. And **IT services** cratered after **Accenture cut its full-year guidance**: **Capgemini fell 8.9% to a six-year low**, with Cancom, Atos and Reply off 2%-6.9% — a read-through that, together with a ~3-4% drop in index-heavyweight SAP, pulled the broad tech complex in the opposite direction from the buoyant semiconductor names.

The macro frame was the hinge. The **Bank of England voted 7-2 to hold Bank Rate at 3.75%**, with two members dissenting for a 25bp hike; it called a rise premature given uncertainty over the Middle East energy shock while keeping its focus on the 2% inflation target — a hold that did little to lift a UK market already pressed by commodity losses. The larger weight came from across the Atlantic: the **Federal Reserve held on June 17 but turned hawkish**, with nine of nineteen policymakers projecting at least one 2026 hike and, in an early imprint from new Chair **Kevin Warsh**, the statement stripping out forward guidance — prompting European investors to raise rate-hike bets and capping risk appetite. The ECB, having raised last week, is priced by LSEG-compiled data for another 25bp by year-end. Stock-specifics rounded out the session: **Edenred surged 17.2%** (the standout) after confirming an investment-fund approach tied to a BC Partners report, while luxury was mixed as risk aversion eased — Hermès +2.1%, Kering +4.4%, but heavyweight LVMH a 1.2% laggard.

## Read-Through

The clean signal for the EU segment is "**relief rally on the continent, commodity-and-rates drag at the index level.**" The US-Iran deal and the resulting slide in oil are unambiguously positive for energy-importing, industrial-heavy Germany and France — hence the DAX's run above 25,000 and the CAC's rebound — but the pan-European STOXX 600 is a different animal: its large energy, mining and pharma weights, plus an SAP/IT-services air-pocket from Accenture's guidance cut, were enough to tip the broad benchmark lower and end its five-day streak. The Euro STOXX 50 sitting near flat, just below its record, is the honest summary of the index tug-of-war.

Two forces bear watching into the next sessions. First, **rates**: the hawkish Warsh-era Fed and a year-end ECB hike still priced mean the European bid is now leaning on the oil/inflation-relief channel rather than on falling discount rates — if the Iran truce frays (nuclear-program talks are only now beginning) or oil reverses, the support weakens quickly. Second, **breadth**: leadership has narrowed to semiconductors and energy-sensitive industrials while pharma, mining, energy, autos and IT services all sold off the same day — a market advancing on a thin column of winners is more fragile than the DAX's record-watch headline suggests. The UK remains the relative funding-short of the bloc: a BoE on hold, a commodity-heavy index, and a domestic consumer signal (Tesco's Q1 sales miss, -1.5%+) all pointing the same way.
