# Global Markets - 2026-06-16

## So what

The June 16 signal was relief without full breadth. Oil and rates moved easier - U.S. crude futures settled down 5.8%, Brent traded below $80 during the morning, and the U.S. 2s10s par-yield spread flattened to +38 bp - but the equity tape did not turn into a clean global risk-on session. [1-3]

The U.S. split is the clearest read-through. The Dow rose to a record 51,999.67, but the S&P 500 fell to 7,511.35, the Nasdaq dropped to 26,376.34, and the PHLX Semiconductor Index slumped nearly 6%. That is rotation out of crowded technology, not a broad fear event. [4-5]

The same pattern showed up overseas. Europe held gains but stayed below euphoria, with the STOXX Europe 600 at 636.00 and the FTSE 100 lagging on energy-major weakness; Asia was a barbell, with the Nikkei and KOSPI positive while the Hang Seng and Shanghai Composite fell. [6-10]

The risk tone stayed calm but cautious. The VIX closed at 16.41, the CNN Fear & Greed Index stood at 41.29 in "Fear," and the Fed calendar still dominated because June 16 was the first day of a two-day FOMC meeting where investors expected a 3.50%-3.75% hold against a 4.2% May CPI backdrop. [1, 11-16]
