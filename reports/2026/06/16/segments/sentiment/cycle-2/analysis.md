# Sentiment Segment Analysis - Cycle 2 (2026-06-16)

## Overview
US risk sentiment cooled at the margin on June 16, 2026, the session after Monday's U.S.-Iran peace-deal melt-up. The pullback was narrow and tech-specific: the Nasdaq fell 1.15% and the S&P 500 slipped 0.57%, while the Dow rose 0.64% to a record high for a second straight day on rotation into industrials and financials. The Cboe Volatility Index (VIX) ticked up just 1.30% to 16.41 and the CNN Fear & Greed Index held at 41.29 ("Fear"), little changed from the prior session. The clean read is mild risk-off rotation, not a fear event — all figures are from the June 16 regular session.

## Drivers
The dominant signal is divergence inside the tape. After Monday's broad rally — when the Nasdaq surged more than 3% on news of a U.S.-Iran deal to end the Middle East war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz — Tuesday saw profit-taking concentrated in this year's biggest winners. The PHLX Semiconductor Index sold off sharply (Investopedia's wrap cited a decline of nearly 6%, erasing Monday's gains), with Intel and AMD each down more than 6% and Nvidia off more than 2%. At the same time, "sleepier" corners such as industrials and financials caught a bid, lifting the Dow to a fresh record. That is the signature of a rotation, not a broad de-risking.

The volatility and sentiment gauges corroborate the narrow read. The VIX rose only modestly, to 16.41, and remained at a historically low, calm level — consistent with a single-sector pullback against a record-setting Dow rather than a market-wide scare. The CNN Fear & Greed Index sat at 41.29, squarely in the "Fear" zone but essentially flat versus the prior session, showing that Monday's relief rally did not flip sentiment into greed and Tuesday's tech wobble did not push it toward panic.

The macro calendar reinforced the wait-and-see posture: the Federal Reserve opened a two-day meeting (a rate decision was due the next day and widely expected to be a hold under new Chair Kevin Warsh), and the G7 summit in France kept the focus on the Iran peace timeline and the Strait of Hormuz reopening, with oil continuing to fall.

## Read-Through
Cycle-2 sentiment is best characterized as constructive-but-cautious. The fear gauges are quiet (VIX in the 16s, Fear & Greed in mild "Fear"), and the Dow's record argues against any broad risk aversion. The risk is concentration: the same crowded semiconductor and momentum names that powered the year's gains are now the source of single-day drawdowns, so headline index moves (especially the Nasdaq) will stay sensitive to that cohort even when breadth is healthy. For the broader pulse, this segment supports a steady risk backdrop into the Fed decision, with the tech/chip complex — not volatility or credit — as the swing factor to watch.

## Correction vs Cycle 1
Cycle 1 (Issue #81 / PR #88) was blocked because it reported the VIX at 16.20 (down 8.4%) as the June 16 close. That figure is the **June 15** close: Yahoo Finance and Investing.com both show VIX closing at 16.20 on June 15 (down 8.37% from 17.68 on June 12) and at 16.41 on June 16 (up 1.30%).
