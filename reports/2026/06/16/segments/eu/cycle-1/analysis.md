## Analysis

European equities paused just shy of record highs on Tuesday, June 16, 2026, taking a breather the day after the global relief rally sparked by a preliminary U.S.-Iran peace agreement. The pan-European **STOXX Europe 600** edged up 1.56 points (+0.25%) to **636.00**, holding just below Monday's record high. Beneath the index-level calm the tape split by geography and sector: gains were broad but measured on the continent, while London lagged.

The clearest divergence was between the energy-light continental bourses and the energy-heavy UK market. **France's CAC 40** rose 0.75% to **8,447.27**, its fourth straight advance and a roughly two-month high, and **Italy's FTSE MIB** led the majors with a ~1.2% jump; **Spain's IBEX 35** added ~0.7%. **Germany's DAX** was the laggard among continental indices, closing essentially flat at **24,910.41** (+0.07%) as financials and industrials — GEA Group rose ~5.1% on a Deutsche Bank upgrade — were offset by weakness in technology and automakers, with BMW and Mercedes-Benz down ~5.2% and ~2.8%. In London the **FTSE 100** rose 0.61% to **10,494.21** on the Investing.com print (Trading Economics similarly had it up about 0.7%), recovering from the prior session's loss but trailing its peers: the benchmark's heavy weighting in energy majors — Shell off ~0.5%, BP off ~0.7% — dragged as crude retreated. AJ Bell's Dan Coatsworth called the FTSE's showing "lukewarm" against European and U.S. counterparts that "charged ahead."

The macro driver was the same one moving Wall Street: the unwinding of the Middle East war-risk premium. President Trump said a preliminary agreement to end the war had been signed by the U.S. and Iran, and **Brent crude fell roughly 4.6%** during the European session toward a three-month low as traders positioned for the Strait of Hormuz to reopen and supply to return. Cheaper energy helped rate-sensitive and cyclical names but punished the oil-and-gas complex that anchors the FTSE 100. Sentiment on the continent stayed measured rather than euphoric, with market participants flagging the lack of concrete operational detail around the Middle East resolution.

In single stocks, **STMicroelectronics** fell ~2.5% after a $1.5 billion dual-tranche convertible bond offering, **Rathbones Group** slumped ~17% after the wealth manager said it would pause onboarding new clients for a year, and **Puma** rose ~1.9% on an HSBC upgrade to Buy; UK defence and banking names (Rolls-Royce ~+2.7%, Babcock ~+2%, HSBC/Lloyds/Barclays ~+1.4–1.8%) supported the FTSE. The post-truce bounce lifted Europe's **year-to-date gain to nearly 8%**, clawing back wartime losses and narrowing the valuation gap with the S&P 500 — though analysts caution that Europe's lighter technology weighting still caps its upside versus the U.S. and Asia. The session reads as orderly consolidation: a continent digesting peace optimism and falling oil, with the UK's energy tilt the main reason it could not keep pace.
