## Analysis

The June 16 macro tape was a digestion of geopolitical de-risking rather than a fresh growth scare. After Monday's sharp rally on news of a U.S.-Iran interim deal, the cleanest macro signal came through energy: U.S. crude oil futures settled down 5.8%, their lowest since early March, while Brent traded as low as $79.96 a barrel during the morning, its first move below $80 since the Iran war began, before edging back near $80.19. That removed a major source of the inflation and supply-shock premium that had built up since the conflict started in late February. [C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-010, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-011]

Rates confirmed the same impulse, with the official Treasury par-yield curve leaning lower across the board. The 2-year par yield eased to 4.05% from 4.07%, the 10-year fell to 4.43% from 4.47% — the lowest June 2026 reading in the official series — and the 30-year slipped to 4.93% from 4.97%. Because the long end rallied a touch more than the front end, the 2s10s par-yield spread flattened by 2 basis points to +38 basis points. This is a mild bull flattening consistent with the market pricing a lower energy-driven inflation risk premium without yet pulling forward any easing. [C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-001, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-002, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-003, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-004]

The policy backdrop dominates the calendar. The Federal Reserve's official 2026 FOMC schedule places June 16 on the first day of a two-day meeting flagged for a Summary of Economic Projections, with the decision due June 17 — the first under new Chair Kevin Warsh. Investors widely expected the Committee to hold the federal funds target range at 3.50%-3.75%, leaving the SEP and Warsh's first press conference as the market's focus rather than the rate itself. [C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-005, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-006]

The equity tape split, which is itself a macro tell. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 0.67% to 52,016.57, a second straight record close, as capital rotated into economically sensitive financials and industrials, while the S&P 500 slipped 0.55% to a preliminary 7,512.44 and the Nasdaq Composite fell 1.15% to 26,382.81 on a sell-off in richly valued technology and chip names. Lower oil and lower yields supported the cyclical, value-tilted parts of the market even as crowded megacap tech took profits ahead of the Fed. [C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-007, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-008, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-009]

The unresolved macro tension is inflation versus the energy relief. The annual CPI rate was 4.2% for May 2026, up from 3.8%, a sticky-inflation backdrop that keeps the Fed biased toward caution even as oil falls. Consistent with that, traders were still pricing a roughly 42% chance of a 25-basis-point hike in December, having scaled back near-term tightening bets on the Iran de-escalation. Net read: financial conditions eased into the Fed meeting through lower oil, lower Treasury yields, and a flatter curve, but the durability of that relief depends on the physical normalization of energy flows through the Strait of Hormuz and on whether the still-elevated CPI keeps a year-end hike alive. [C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-012, C-2026-06-16-MACRO-C1-013]
