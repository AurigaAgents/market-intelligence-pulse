# Fragile Stabilization, Tech Still Bleeding - 2026-06-24

## The hook

June 24 was a bifurcated stabilization. The Dow rose 0.4% to 51,848.90, the Russell 2000 gained 0.4% to 2,986.63, and nearly two out of every three S&P 500 stocks advanced — but the S&P 500 still fell 0.1% to 7,358.22 and the Nasdaq dropped 0.4% to 25,476.64, because Microsoft (-2.3%) and Oracle (-4.6%) dragged the cap-weighted composite negative. [C-2026-06-24-US-C2-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-24-US-C2-BREADTH, C-2026-06-24-US-C2-TECH-DRAG]

## Why it mattered

The stabilization was cross-regional. The KOSPI surged 3.26% to 8,471.02 on bargain hunting after Tuesday's roughly 10% crash, the CAC 40 snapped a three-session losing streak with a 0.54% gain, and the FTSE 100 added 0.31%. But the Nikkei fell for a second day (-0.88% to 69,174.97), the DAX lost 0.73% on the Rheinmetall shock, and the Nasdaq extended its losing streak to three. [C-2026-06-24-ASIA-C1-KOSPI-REBOUND, C-2026-06-24-ASIA-C1-NIKKEI-DECLINE, C-2026-06-24-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE]

The cross-asset driver was crude oil. Brent slid about 4% to near $73.97/bbl, a four-month low, as US-Iran de-escalation and Hormuz reopening eased supply fears. Treasury yields fell 5-10 bp across the curve — 10Y to 4.41% — and the 5-year breakeven dropped to 2.20% (lowest this year). Gold slipped below $4,000/oz on a spot basis. [C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-OIL, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-GOLD-SILVER]

But the hawkish-Fed counterforce is intact. The dollar rose for a sixth straight session to a 13-month high near 101.6, the market prices a second 2026 Fed hike, and the BOJ summary affirmed further tightening. Thursday's May PCE print (consensus +4.1% YoY headline, a three-year high) is the next decision point. [C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-DOLLAR, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-FED-PCE, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-BOJ]

## The caveat

This was not a clean risk-on rebound. The VIX eased 4.41% to 18.63 and the vol curve normalized, but the Fear & Greed Index stayed in fear at 27-28 and the S&P 500 and Nasdaq fell for a third consecutive session. The AAII survey and latest flow readings still predate the selloff, so positioning confirmation has not arrived. [C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-VOLATILITY, C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-FEAR-GREED, C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-SYNTHESIS]

## Our take

The base case is fragile stabilization pending two catalysts. Micron's earnings after the close tests whether the semiconductor complex finds a floor; the May PCE release on Thursday tests whether the hawkish-Fed leg intensifies or the oil-led disinflation impulse prevails. If both clear favourably, the rotation bid (Dow, Russell 2000, KOSPI, European cyclicals) can extend. If either fails, the tech losing streak becomes the dominant signal again. [C-2026-06-24-US-C2-CHIP-MICRON, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-FED-PCE, C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-SYNTHESIS]

**Conviction:** Medium. The cross-region breadth improvement and oil-led macro relief are real, but the tech/AI overhang is unresolved and fresh positioning data are still missing.
