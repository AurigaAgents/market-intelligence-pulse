## Read-Through
The June 24 sentiment regime is "fragile stabilization, tech still bleeding." June 23's unambiguous risk-off — all four major indexes down, VIX +12.79%, Fear & Greed at 28 — gave way on June 24 to a mixed close in which the Dow and Russell 2000 each rose 0.4%, AP reported more advancers than decliners within the S&P 500, and the VIX eased 4.41% to 18.63 with the VIX9D-to-VIX spread restoring a normal upward slope. But the S&P 500 still fell 0.1% and the Nasdaq dropped 0.4% as Microsoft and other tech giants dragged, the Fear & Greed Index stayed in fear at 27-28, and the AAII survey and latest flow readings remain pre-selloff.

For the full Pulse, that makes sentiment a less acute but unresolved risk-off signal. The key question is whether the rotation bid (Dow +0.4%, Russell +0.4%, Brent toward prewar levels, VIX easing) is strong enough to absorb the continuing tech/AI overhang — a question Micron's earnings after the close and the AAII survey due Thursday June 25 will help answer. The fact that the VIX9D fell back below the VIX and the intraday ranges narrowed into the close argue for stabilization; the fact that the Fear & Greed Index stayed in fear and tech couldn't bounce argues for more downside risk. [C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-SYNTHESIS]
