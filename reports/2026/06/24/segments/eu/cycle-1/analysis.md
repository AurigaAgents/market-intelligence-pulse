The pan-European STOXX Europe 600 edged up 0.08% to 635.16 (Reuters: closed 0.1% higher), France's CAC 40 was the standout with a 0.54% gain to 8,385.49 that snapped a three-session losing streak, and the UK's FTSE 100 added 0.31% to a precise 10,461.63. Beneath the flat index headline, the tape rotated out of defence, commodities and banks and into real estate and consumer discretionary, as US-Iran de-escalation pushed oil to multi-week lows.

## Session Status
Wednesday, June 24 was a normal European cash-equity trading day, and the segment reports a verified close rather than a holiday read. The session is evidenced by live closing prints with full volume: the Investors Chronicle/LSEG FTSE 100 daily table shows a June 24 row (open 10,429.02, high 10,469.33, low 10,406.95, close 10,461.63) on volume of 960,305,976 shares, and the Trading Economics Euro Area, Germany and France pages carry dated June 24 closes, corroborated by the Reuters European close wrap published mid-session New York time.

There is no cross-market calendar asymmetry on this report date. Both European and US venues traded a normal regular session, so the European daily moves are measured against the immediately prior Tuesday, June 23 close. After Tuesday's broad tech-led selloff, Wednesday was a stabilization day: indices hugged the flatline at the headline level while the action concentrated in two large single-stock stories and a clean sector rotation.

The pan-European benchmark essentially tread water as gains in real estate and consumer discretionary offset weakness in commodities, defence and banks.

Trading Economics framed it as the CAC "rose 0.5% to close at 8,385 ... as gains in the luxury sector supported the index"; the move is corroborated by the dated Euro Area cross-index table. - **FTSE 100** - the one precise official close: 10,461.63, up 32.78 points (+0.31%) from Tuesday's 10,428.85 on the Investors Chronicle/LSEG historical daily row (data provided by LSEG, delayed at least 15 minutes). London was the day's relative outperformer.

## Session Drivers
The day was a muted, stabilizing session shaped more by idiosyncratic corporate news than a single macro theme. The macro backdrop was constructive: investors assessed de-escalation in the US-Iran negotiations and signs of improving shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, which pushed Brent crude to its lowest level since the US-Israeli war on Iran began as supply-disruption fears eased. Lower oil and retreating eurozone sovereign yields lifted rate-sensitive and consumer-discretionary names, even as commodity-linked sectors fell. On the data front, German Ifo business morale rose in June, with companies more positive about their current situation than at any point in nearly two years, and traders continued to price another 25-basis-point ECB rate hike by year-end per LSEG-compiled data. The dominant stock-specific shock was Germany scrapping plans to build six F126 frigates over delays and expected cost overruns - a contract Rheinmetall was tipped to win - switching instead to smaller Meko A-200 frigates from Thyssenkrupp's TKMS. Tech sentiment was cautious into US chipmaker Micron's quarterly results, with the European tech sector reversing early gains.

## Sector Rotation
The tape was a clean rotation out of defence, commodities and banks into real estate and consumer discretionary. On the leader side, the real estate sector rose 3%, led by Segro's 17.4% surge after US-based Prologis took its 16.6 billion dollar bid public following Segro's rejection - the warehouse landlord's biggest intraday jump since March 2009 - and consumer-discretionary names rebounded, with Adidas, LVMH, Hermes and Inditex rising up to 3% on Trading Economics data. On the laggard side, commodity-linked sectors were the biggest weights: miners fell 2.5% and energy 2.3% tracking lower metals and oil prices, aerospace and defence lost 0.8% (dragged by Rheinmetall), industrials eased 0.2%, and technology reversed gains to fall 0.3% after the prior session's near-five-month-worst drop. Banks fell as markets took profits. Sector and single-stock percentages are approximate and carried at medium confidence; the index-level closes are high confidence.

## Standout Movers
The day's epicentre was Rheinmetall, which fell sharply - Reuters put it at -18.7%, its biggest one-day drop on record (Trading Economics component -14.79% to 996.60; intraday reports ranged -13% to -19%) - after Germany scrapped its F126 frigate programme; its counterpart Thyssenkrupp TKMS jumped 16%. The other outsized move was Segro, up 17.4% on the Prologis bid. Luxury and aerospace led the gainers: L'Oreal +2.83% (helped by a Jefferies target-price raise to 328 euros from 323), Safran +2.20%, Air Liquide +1.99%, LVMH +1.92%, Hermes +1.50%, Inditex +1.12%, Kering about +1% and Airbus +0.95%. On the downside, TotalEnergies lost 1.84% on lower oil, Thales fell about 1.2% on the European defence drag, and Argenx dropped about 7% on a myositis drug-trial setback. Chipmakers were mixed by basis: the Reuters cash close had Infineon -1.2%, BE Semiconductor -1.3% and ASML -0.5%, while the later Trading Economics CFD snapshot showed Infineon +1.29% and ASML -0.45% - a cash-close-versus-CFD divergence that is explicitly disclosed. Single-stock percentages are carried at medium confidence.
