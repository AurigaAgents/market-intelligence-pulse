## Overview
US equities closed mixed on Wednesday, June 24, 2026, as an early rebound from a bruising two-day technology selloff faded into the close. The Associated Press closing-index ledger reported the S&P 500 down 7.24 points (0.1%) to 7,358.22, the Nasdaq Composite down 110.40 points (0.4%) to 25,476.64, the Dow Jones Industrial Average up 182.06 points (0.4%) to 51,848.90, and the Russell 2000 up 11.15 points (0.4%) to 2,986.63. Daily moves are measured against the prior regular session of Tuesday, June 23. The split — a higher Dow against a lower Nasdaq — again reflected technology weakness, but the internals were healthier than Tuesday's rout: the AP noted more stocks rose than fell within the S&P 500 even as the index slipped, with losses in tech giants including Microsoft accounting for the drag. This is a normal trading session, so the segment carries verified closes plus the day's drivers, not a no-session holiday read. The marquee catalyst — Micron Technology's fiscal third-quarter earnings — landed after the closing bell and is therefore not yet in this cycle's data.

## Session Drivers
The day was a fade. After two sessions of heavy technology selling, US stocks opened higher and pushed up through late morning — Investing.com clocked the S&P 500 up about 0.7% near 7,416.86 and the Dow up about 1.1% near 52,215.29, within striking distance of an intraday record. Those gains did not hold. Investopedia, updating in late trading, framed the S&P 500 and Nasdaq as on track to close lower for a third consecutive session, and both did: the S&P 500 finished down 0.1% and the Nasdaq down 0.4%, while the Dow's lighter technology weighting let it hold a 0.4% gain. Yahoo Finance attributed the renewed selling in technology to worries about lofty valuations, heavy AI capital spending, and coming interest-rate hikes, which prompted profit-taking in the high-flying AI-linked names that had led the market to records into early June.

## Breadth: A Healthier Tape Than the Headline
Unlike Tuesday's narrow rout, Wednesday's internals were constructive. The AP reported that more stocks rose than fell within the S&P 500 even though the cap-weighted index slipped 0.1%, because the losses were concentrated in a handful of large technology names — the AP singled out Microsoft. The Dow's 0.4% gain and the Russell 2000's 0.4% rise underscored the rotation: money favored the tech-lighter and more domestically exposed corners of the market. The Russell 2000 closed at 2,986.63, recovering part of Tuesday's loss but remaining below the 3,000 level it had cleared for the first time on June 22.

## Semiconductors and Mega-Cap Technology
The pressure point remained chips and the largest growth stocks. Per Investopedia's late-session update, Micron slipped about 2% ahead of its earnings, SanDisk fell about 3% and Western Digital about 5%, while Magnificent Seven members Nvidia, Microsoft, and Tesla each fell more than 1%. AI chip designer Cerebras Systems tumbled about 19% after its first earnings report as a public company guided current-quarter gross margins to narrow by up to 10 percentage points — a reminder that the market is repricing AI-hardware economics, not just momentum. These single-stock percentages are intraday or late-session snapshots and are carried at medium confidence; they may differ from final closes.

## Micron: The Catalyst After the Bell
The session's biggest unknown sat outside it. Micron Technology was scheduled to report fiscal third-quarter results (for the quarter ended May) after the closing bell, a print Wall Street treated as a litmus test for AI-driven memory demand. Yahoo Finance noted the stock had rallied more than 250% this year and extended its losses into the report after falling about 13% on Tuesday; Investing.com put consensus at roughly $19.92 in EPS on about $34.66 billion of revenue. Because the results land after the close, the earnings outcome is not part of this cycle's verified data; only the pre-earnings positioning is, and it is carried at medium confidence.

## Macro: Oil, Yields, and Thursday's PCE
The macro tape leaned disinflationary on the day. Oil extended its slide to the lowest level since the war with Iran began: the AP put Brent crude down 3.8%, and Yahoo Finance and Investopedia live updates showed Brent near $73 and West Texas Intermediate near $70 (about -4.4%), just above pre-war prices, as tankers resumed transiting the Strait of Hormuz — even as Iran and Oman were reported to be discussing tolls on the waterway. The AP said the oil decline helped ease inflation concerns, and Treasury yields fell: Investopedia put the 10-year near 4.41%, down from about 4.50% a day earlier, with the 2-year near 4.15%, close to its highest this year. Attention now turns to Thursday's PCE inflation report, which Investopedia said economists expect to show headline inflation of 4.1% and core inflation of 3.4% over the 12 months ended May. Oil dollar levels, intraday percentages, and yields are mutable live-page readings and are carried at medium confidence; the AP Brent -3.8% figure is the closing-ledger value.

## Index Reconstitution
Separately, S&P Dow Jones Indices announced changes to its marquee benchmarks, effective Monday, June 29: Alphabet will replace Verizon in the Dow Jones Industrial Average, and Honeywell Aerospace — being spun off from Honeywell — will replace Conagra Brands in the S&P 500.
