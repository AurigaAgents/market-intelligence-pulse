## Overview
US equities closed mixed on Wednesday, June 24, 2026, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite extending their losing streak to a third consecutive session while the Dow and Russell 2000 gained. The S&P 500 fell 7.24 points (-0.1%) to 7,358.22 and the Nasdaq fell 110.40 points (-0.4%) to 25,476.64, but the Dow rose 182.06 points (+0.4%) to 51,848.90 and the Russell 2000 added 11.15 points (+0.4%) to 2,986.63. The divergence between the cap-weighted S&P 500 and internal breadth was stark: nearly two out of every three stocks in the S&P 500 gained ground, yet the index still fell, because Microsoft alone subtracted enough points to drag the composite negative.

The session's signal is bifurcation. Large-cap technology and energy weighed on the indexes; everything else mostly rose. Microsoft fell 2.3% and Oracle slumped 4.6%, carrying the heaviest weights. Oil prices plunged to their lowest level since before the US-Iran war began — Brent settled near $73.80 per barrel — dragging energy equities down with it. Treasury yields fell in sympathy, with the 10-year dropping to 4.40% from 4.50%. Homebuilders surged on favourable legislation: KB Home jumped 16.7% and D.R. Horton gained 6.7%. Gold settled at $4,008.80 (-3.4%), briefly trading below $4,000 intraday.

The macro focus now turns to Thursday's PCE inflation report, with economists expecting a 4.1% year-over-year headline reading, which would be the highest in three years. Micron Technology, which ended down 0.3% ahead of its earnings release after the bell, reports with FactSet consensus at $20.83 EPS on $35.75B revenue.

## Session Status
Wednesday, June 24, 2026 was a regular cash-equity and derivatives trading day across all referenced US venues. There were no market holidays. The session was the third consecutive decline for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite, following Monday's semiconductor-led sell-off and Tuesday's broad technology rout. On Tuesday June 23, the S&P 500 fell 1.4% and the Nasdaq Composite fell 2.2% as memory and chip stocks crumbled; the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) ended that session 7% lower. June 24 therefore served as a stabilization test: the headline indexes continued to slip under tech-weight pressure, but the broad market — the Russell 2000's +0.4% gain and the roughly two-thirds advance rate inside the S&P 500 — held up better than the cap-weighted index suggests.

## Index Closes
All four major index closes are anchored to the Associated Press close summary, with independent corroboration from CNBC's close-section reporting:

- **S&P 500** — 7,358.22, down 7.24 points (-0.1%) from the June 23 close of 7,365.46. CNBC independently confirmed the 7,358.22 level at -0.10%. Week-to-date: -1.9%. Year-to-date: +7.5%.
- **Dow Jones Industrial Average** — 51,848.90, up 182.06 points (+0.4%) from 51,666.84. CNBC independently confirmed the 51,848.90 level at +182.06 (+0.35%). Week-to-date: +0.6%. Year-to-date: +7.9%.
- **Nasdaq Composite** — 25,476.64, down 110.40 points (-0.4%) from 25,587.04. CNBC independently confirmed the 25,476.64 level at -0.43%. Week-to-date: -3.9%. Year-to-date: +9.6%.
- **Russell 2000** — 2,986.63, up 11.15 points (+0.4%) from 2,975.48. Week-to-date: +0.2%. Year-to-date: +20.3%.

The AP and CNBC close figures agree to the cent on S&P 500, Dow, and Nasdaq closing levels. The AP provides the sole sourced Russell 2000 close.

## Breadth and Internal Divergence
The single most notable structural feature of the session was the divergence between the cap-weighted S&P 500 and its internals. AP reported that "nearly 2 out of every 3 stocks" in the S&P 500 gained ground even as the index fell 0.1%. This means the decline was almost entirely attributable to the largest market-cap constituents — principally Microsoft (-2.3%), which AP identified as the heaviest weight on the market, and Oracle (-4.6%). AP explicitly noted that the same pattern occurred on Tuesday: "tech stocks pulled the market lower despite broader gains elsewhere." The Russell 2000's +0.4% gain is consistent with the positive internal breadth reading: smaller companies — which dominate the Russell 2000 and the S&P 500 equal-weight index but are underrepresented in the cap-weighted S&P 500 — rose on the day.

## Technology Drag
Large-cap technology was the dominant negative driver:

- **Microsoft** -2.3% — AP: "the heaviest weight on the market"
- **Oracle** -4.6%
- **Alphabet** -0.2% — the company is replacing Verizon in the Dow on Monday, becoming the fifth Magnificent 7 member in the price-weighted index

AP framed these as "declines for several influential tech heavyweights" that "pulled the broader market lower." Analyst commentary reflected the valuation-stretch narrative: Rick Gardner, chief investment officer at RGA Investments, called the pullback "a healthy recalibration of expectations" given that "earnings expectations for tech stocks are high, creating a more difficult bar to clear when earnings season re-starts in July." Jason Vaillancourt of Columbia Threadneedle wrote that "the next phase of the AI investment cycle is beginning to collide with market discipline."

## Oil and Energy
Oil prices fell to their lowest level since before the US-Iran war began, as the two nations negotiate a possible end to the conflict:

- **Brent crude** — a disclosed vendor discrepancy: AP reported a settle of $73.87 (-3.8%), while CNBC reported $73.74 (-4.33%). Both sources agree Brent is at its lowest since before the US and Israel launched airstrikes against Iran at the end of February. The AP and CNBC figures reflect different settlement-reporting conventions; both are retained with explicit disclosure.
- **WTI crude** — $70.34, down 3.9% (AP) or 3.92% (CNBC), the lowest since early March.

Energy equities fell broadly. Exxon Mobil lost 2% and Chevron lost 2.6% (AP). CNBC added that ConocoPhillips and SLB also declined more than 2%, and the Energy Select Sector SPDR ETF (XLE) fell more than 1%.

## Rates
Treasury yields mostly fell as oil prices declined:

- **10-year Treasury** — 4.40%, down from 4.50% late Tuesday (AP close). CNBC noted the 10-year "dropped below 4.5%."
- **2-year Treasury** — 4.15%, down from 4.16% (AP close).

Despite the decline, AP noted yields remain "elevated from earlier in the year, especially the 2-year Treasury, which more closely tracks anticipated action from the Federal Reserve." CME Group data shows Wall Street forecasting at least one rate hike by December. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, in last week's meeting, was described as "emphatic about the central bank's desire to achieve price stability."

## Chips and Micron
Chip and memory stocks stabilized after Tuesday's punishing session but still closed lower:

- **Micron Technology (MU)** — ended down 0.3% (CNBC close). The stock came off its session lows as investors positioned ahead of the fiscal Q3 earnings release after the bell. Analysts polled by FactSet see earnings of $20.83 per share on revenue of $35.75 billion. Micron ended Tuesday at $1,051.77 after hitting an all-time high on Monday.
- **Sandisk (SNDK)** — ended down 2.5% (CNBC close). Both Micron and Sandisk tumbled 13% in the prior session.
- **VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH)** — ended marginally lower (CNBC close).

Cerebras Systems (CBRS), the AI chip designer that went public last month, tumbled after its first post-IPO earnings report on Tuesday evening. CNBC reported the stock fell roughly 15% on Wednesday after the company guided for a drop in gross margins despite beating revenue expectations. Investopedia's late-session reading showed the decline near 19%. Wall Street analysts at TD Cowen, Morgan Stanley, and UBS all raised price targets and maintained bullish ratings. This is carried at context level and not as a numeric close claim, since the exact closing percentage is not consistently reported across sources.

## Homebuilders and Standout Movers
Homebuilders were among the biggest winners after Congress approved legislation beneficial to the industry:

- **KB Home** +16.7% — surged after reporting a fiscal Q2 revenue beat of $1.11B vs $1.10B consensus. CNBC noted the stock was on pace for its best day since January 2022.
- **D.R. Horton** +6.7%.

Wendy's (WEN) surged over 40% at peaks on a viral Reddit meme-stock rally before paring gains, a signal of lingering risk-on energy in pockets of the retail market.

## Macro Context and Looking Ahead
Thursday's PCE inflation report is the next major risk event. Economists surveyed by Dow Jones Newswires and The Wall Street Journal expect the headline PCE price index to show prices rose 4.1% over the 12 months ending in May — up from 3.8% in April and the highest since 2023. Core PCE (excluding food and energy) is expected at 3.4% year-over-year. The May inflation print largely reflects gasoline price surges from the Iran war that have since begun to ease, but a hot core reading would reinforce the case for a Fed rate hike by year-end.

Gold fell 3.4% to settle at $4,008.80 an ounce (AP close), briefly trading below $4,000 intraday for the first time since November. Bitcoin dropped to $59,023.98, its lowest level since October 2024. The US dollar index was recently up 0.2% at 101.60 (Investopedia).

JPMorgan raised its year-end S&P 500 target to 7,800 on Wednesday, citing "unprecedented" earnings strength and progress toward a US-Iran peace deal. The new target represents roughly 6% upside from Tuesday's close.
