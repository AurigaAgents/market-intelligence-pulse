# Global Markets - 2026-06-24

## So what

June 24 was a fragile stabilization after Tuesday's global technology rout. The split was everywhere: the Dow rose 182.06 points, or 0.4%, to 51,848.90 and the Russell 2000 added 0.4% to 2,986.63, while the S&P 500 fell 7.24 points, or 0.1%, to 7,358.22 and the Nasdaq dropped 110.40 points, or 0.4%, to 25,476.64. Nearly two out of every three S&P 500 stocks gained ground despite the index decline. [C-2026-06-24-US-C2-EQUITY-CLOSE, C-2026-06-24-US-C2-BREADTH]

Asia set the stabilization tone first. South Korea's KOSPI surged 3.26% to 8,471.02 on bargain hunting after Tuesday's roughly 10% crash, while Japan's Nikkei 225 fell 0.88% to 69,174.97 for a second consecutive decline. Greater China edged higher: the Hang Seng rose 0.33% and the Shanghai Composite added 0.11%. [C-2026-06-24-ASIA-C1-REGIONAL-CLOSE, C-2026-06-24-ASIA-C1-KOSPI-REBOUND]

The macro engine was oil. Brent slid about 4% to near $73.97/bbl, a four-month low, as US-Iran de-escalation and Strait of Hormuz reopening eased supply fears. Treasury yields fell 5-10 bp across the curve — 10Y to 4.41% — and inflation expectations dropped to the year's lows. But the dollar still rose for a sixth straight day to a 13-month high near 101.6 as the market prices a second 2026 Fed hike. [C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-OIL, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-TREASURY-CURVE, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-DOLLAR]

Europe was mixed and idiosyncratic. The STOXX 600 edged up 0.08% to 635.16, the CAC 40 rose 0.54% to 8,385.49 (snapping a three-session losing streak), and the FTSE 100 added 0.31% to 10,461.63. The DAX fell 0.73% to 24,712.70, dragged by Rheinmetall's roughly -18.7% plunge after Germany scrapped its F126 frigate programme; Segro surged 17.4% on the Prologis bid. [C-2026-06-24-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE, C-2026-06-24-EU-C1-MOVERS]

Sentiment shifted toward stabilization but stayed in fear. The VIX eased 4.41% to 18.63 and the vol curve normalized, but the Fear & Greed Index stayed at 27 (fear) and the S&P 500 and Nasdaq fell for a third consecutive session. Micron earnings and the May PCE report on Thursday are the next tests. [C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-VOLATILITY, C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-FEAR-GREED, C-2026-06-24-SENTIMENT-C1-SYNTHESIS]

## Cross-Segment Read

The cleanest signal is bifurcation. Cap-weighted technology dragged headline indexes lower while equal-weight breadth and small-caps were positive — a pattern visible across US, European, and Asian sessions. The KOSPI's +3.26% rebound, the Dow's +0.4%, and the CAC 40's +0.54% show stabilization was real; the Nasdaq's -0.4%, the DAX's -0.73% and the Nikkei's -0.88% show technology and export-heavy indexes remained under pressure. [C-2026-06-24-US-C2-BREADTH, C-2026-06-24-ASIA-C1-REGIONAL-CLOSE, C-2026-06-24-EU-C1-INDEX-CLOSE]

The macro rotation was driven by oil's collapse. Brent's slide pulled yields and breakevens lower, but a hawkish Fed keeps the dollar at a 13-month high and the market pricing a second hike. Thursday's May PCE print (consensus +4.1% YoY) is the next decision point. [C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-OIL, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-DOLLAR, C-2026-06-24-MACRO-C1-FED-PCE]
