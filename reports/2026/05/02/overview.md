## So What

Global risk appetite is still alive, but the tape no longer deserves a simple “all clear.” The S&P 500 closed at a record 7,230.12 and the Nasdaq Composite at 25,114.44, powered by Apple’s $111.2B March-quarter revenue, iPhone 17 demand language, and a fresh $100B buyback authorization. Volatility compressed toward 17, CNN Fear & Greed sat in Greed, and equity put/call stayed call-heavy at 0.46.

The caution is in the plumbing. The Dow fell on the same day the Nasdaq rallied, US yields rose on the week even as they eased Friday, Brent still closed near $108 with a war premium, gold held around $4,592, and the dollar weakness was mostly a yen-intervention event rather than a broad easing impulse. Europe’s live session was mostly London-only because of May 1 holidays. Asia was even thinner: Tokyo printed a modest gain, while Korea, Hong Kong, and mainland China were closed. This is a records-with-hedges market, not a synchronized global breakout.

## Risk Dashboard

1. **USD/JPY is the load-bearing macro line.** Suspected MoF/BoJ intervention after USD/JPY breached 160 pushed the pair as low as roughly 155.5 before settling near 157. That stabilized Asian FX and mechanically weakened DXY, but it did not close the rate differential. A clean retest of 160 would challenge Tokyo’s credibility and likely tighten global risk conditions.

2. **Oil relief is real but incomplete.** Brent’s drop to $108.17 helped equities and reduced immediate inflation fear, but the war premium remains. The level that matters is not the daily decline; it is whether Brent can hold below $105 and then move toward $100. A reversal above $110 would put the stagflation trade back on top.

3. **Central banks are paused, not dovish.** The Fed held 3.50–3.75% with unusual dissent, the ECB kept its deposit rate at 2.00% while eurozone inflation printed 3.0%, the BoE held 3.75% with a hike dissent, and the BoJ held 0.75% without backing currency intervention with rate support. Markets are pricing patience, not synchronized easing.

4. **Breadth is the US warning label.** The S&P and Nasdaq records are legitimate, but Apple and AI-adjacent leaders are doing a lot of the work. The Dow’s red close and the 10Y at 4.39% say cyclicals and duration are not receiving the same blessing. Russell 2000’s +0.9% weekly gain softens the warning; it does not erase it.

5. **Sentiment is not euphoric enough to fade blindly.** VIX near 17, CNN Greed, and put/call at 0.46 show risk appetite. AAII at 38.1% bullish and 39.7% bearish shows retail conviction is unstable rather than fully converted. That mix can extend rallies, but it makes a catalyst-driven VIX pop more asymmetric.

## Cross-Segment Synthesis

The common pattern is **risk-on price action with defensive macro confirmation**. Equities are buying the earnings and AI-capex story. Commodities and FX are buying insurance. Gold near $4,592 and silver above $74 are not normal companions to record US indices unless investors also want currency, inflation, or geopolitical protection. Copper’s $5.93 print still carries an AI/data-center and supply-stress bid, while natural gas at $2.78 is now a tightening-balance story after a below-normal 79 Bcf storage injection and lower production.

Europe is the clearest stagflation expression. The STOXX/DAX Apr 30 rebound was a relief trade after oil backed off and central banks avoided an immediate hawkish surprise. But Bund 10Y near 3.10% is the macro pin: above 3.20%, pressure returns to duration-sensitive equities and periphery spreads; below 3.00%, the relief trade can broaden.

Asia is a holiday-thinned FX story. The corrected Korea data matters: KOSPI did not make a May 1 fresh close; Korea was shut, and the latest close was 6,598.87 after a 1.38% reversal on Apr 30. Tokyo’s 0.38% gain despite a stronger yen is constructive, but TOPIX’s flat finish shows narrow leadership. If USD/JPY holds below 157 without repeated official action, the region stabilizes. If it retests 160, currency risk becomes the week’s dominant cross-asset factor.

## Conditions

Net conditions are **risk-on with a defensive macro overlay**. The bullish case: Apple validates hardware demand, AI capex keeps pulling semis and mega-cap tech higher, Brent breaks toward $100, and Tokyo defends the yen without forcing an equity unwind. The bearish case: Brent reclaims $110, USD/JPY retests 160, Bunds push above 3.20%, or mega-cap breadth narrows further. Until one breaks, chase strength only with defined risk.
