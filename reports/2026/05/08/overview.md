# Markets Overview — 2026-05-08

## So What

Markets are not trading a clean panic, but they are no longer rewarding the first layer of Iran ceasefire optimism either. The visible tape is Risk-Off-Lite: continental Europe and China/Hong Kong slipped, Japan took profits near records, and Thursday's US close reversed from fresh highs. The deeper cross-asset message is more constructive and more complicated. A strong US payrolls print did not push Treasury yields higher, the dollar stayed near 52-week lows, EUR/USD pressed toward 1.18 on hawkish ECB repricing, and gold plus copper rallied together. The day's real signal is a market rotating away from pure equity beta and toward policy-divergence, hard-asset, and energy-circuit-breaker trades.

## Risk Dashboard

1. **Brent $100 remains the circuit breaker.** Asia's approved report had Brent near $100.10; macro had it near $101.20. That timestamp spread is normal, but the level is decisive. Sustained trade above $103 would revive CPI and margin pressure; a break below $100 would restore the de-escalation trade.

2. **US yields are refusing the hot-data script.** A payrolls beat with lower 2Y/10Y yields is a meaningful asymmetry. If yields keep falling on strong data, equities can treat growth as durable and inflation as contained. If the long end snaps back above 4.40%, the soft-landing cushion weakens.

3. **Europe has the tightest policy/geopolitics squeeze.** The euro near 1.18 and more than 50 bp of ECB tightening priced into year-end create a stagflation-lite setup when oil risk rises. Continental indices need STOXX 600 back above 620 to show the squeeze is absorbed.

4. **Korea is the positive outlier.** KOSPI holding green after a massive week says the AI/semi bid is still alive. If Korea rolls over with Japan, the Asia story becomes broad de-risking; if Korea holds 7,400-7,500, the region is consolidating, not failing.

5. **Hard assets are not confirming equity complacency.** Gold near record territory and copper up strongly are not defensive in the same way. Together they argue for a market buying real assets against a weak-dollar/policy-divergence regime.

6. **Sentiment data require a footnote today.** Fear & Greed and put/call are usable; the SPX/RUT breadth claim from the rejected analyst is not. The correct read is mega-cap leadership with modest small-cap underperformance, not an outright small-cap risk-off break.

## Cross-Segment Synthesis

The common driver is not simply Iran. Hormuz headlines explain why oil stayed bid, why Europe underperformed the FTSE, and why Asia entered the weekend cautious. But the cleaner regime signal comes from the assets that should have moved differently if this were only geopolitical fear: US yields fell after strong payrolls, the dollar stayed weak, copper rose alongside gold, and EUR/USD firmed on ECB repricing. That constellation says investors are trading policy divergence and real-asset scarcity as much as shipping-lane risk.

Equities are therefore bifurcated. The broad index tape is tired after a record-setting stretch: US indices reversed Thursday, Europe gave back part of its relief rally, and Asia cooled after Japan/Korea strength. But the weakness is controlled. VIX is still sub-18, Fear & Greed is below extreme territory, and Korea plus US mega-cap leadership have not broken. This is distribution at the margin, not liquidation.

The market's practical map is simple: **Risk-Off-Lite in equities, Risk-On in hard assets, Neutral-to-constructive in rates.** If Brent falls back below $100 while DXY breaks 97.50, the hard-asset/weak-dollar trade can coexist with renewed equity upside. If Brent pushes through $103-$110 and US yields stop easing, the same positioning becomes vulnerable because Europe, small caps, and Asia importers would all be carrying the shock at once.
