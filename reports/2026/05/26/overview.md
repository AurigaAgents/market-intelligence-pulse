# Global Markets - May 26, 2026

## Opening Take

The so what from the approved bundle is that markets are still trading a peace dividend, but now with live-fire risk back on the screen. Monday's US-Iran deal hopes drove the biggest macro impulse: oil's war premium fell hard, equity futures improved, and Europe rotated into banks and airlines. Tuesday's later files show the catch. Fresh US strikes in southern Iran produced a mild oil rebound and kept Asia mixed rather than euphoric. The global stance is therefore risk-on, but conditional: investors are paying for lower energy stress before the diplomacy has actually settled the Hormuz and Iran tail risk.

## What Drove The Day

**1. Oil still sets the macro temperature.** The cleanest cross-asset signal is crude. WTI at $91.68 and Brent at $97.88 remain far below early-May stress after Monday's peace-driven drop. Tuesday's small bounce matters because it followed new US strikes. A confirmed Iran deal pulls inflation pressure lower; failed talks can return the war premium quickly.

**2. The US tape wants confirmation.** US cash markets were closed Monday, so the approved US file mixes Friday closes with Tuesday futures. The setup is constructive: S&P futures +0.7%, Nasdaq 100 futures +1.1%, Russell 2000 strength, and the 10-year yield at 4.49%. Earnings remain a stabilizer, with the US file citing projected S&P 500 earnings growth of 23% this year. The risk is that cash trading reopens into headlines that are less clean than the first peace impulse.

**3. Europe was the clearest expression.** Monday's rally favored Santander, BBVA, Deutsche Bank, Air France-KLM, and Lufthansa while Rheinmetall, Hensoldt, and Leonardo faded. Tuesday consolidated, with STOXX 50 down 0.3% and STOXX 600 near 630.5. The bond signal is less friendly: Bunds at 2.97% say 3.0% euro-area inflation still blocks a full rates-relief story.

**4. Asia refused to rubber-stamp optimism.** The Nikkei's 0.25% pullback to 64,996.09 was profit-taking after a record close above 65,000, not trend failure. But Hong Kong was flat, Shanghai slipped, and USD/JPY near 159 leaves intervention risk on watch. For energy importers, oil headlines matter more than local growth today.

## Cross-Segment Synthesis

The global stance is **risk-on with a strike-risk discount**. The bullish case is visible across the bundle: US futures improved, Europe rotated into peace beneficiaries, oil remains far below early-May panic levels, the US 10-year yield eased, and headline sentiment is still in greed. If diplomacy produces a durable Iran framework, the market can extend the move through lower oil, lower inflation expectations, and a wider runway for central banks.

The bearish case is just as simple. The reports do not show a completed peace settlement; they show markets reacting to its rising probability. Meanwhile, the fresh US strikes, firm Bund yields, ECB inflation trap, yen weakness, and historic gold demand all say investors are not actually relaxed. The strongest interpretation is not "all clear." It is a market paying up for de-escalation while quietly buying insurance against failure.

Next session, the clean test is whether oil stays subdued while US cash trading confirms the futures signal. If Brent remains below $100, US 10Y stays near 4.5%, and VIX holds below 17, the peace dividend can broaden. If crude rebounds with yields, May 26 will look like a fragile headline rally rather than a durable risk reset.
